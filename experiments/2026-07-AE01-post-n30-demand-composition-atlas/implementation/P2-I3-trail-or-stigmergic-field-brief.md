## 1. Role and authority

This brief supplies the semantic and observational center for P2-I3. It asks
what a trail or stigmergic field could mean geometrically and ecologically,
what dynamic relation must distinguish it from N30-style static trace
persistence, how several notions of distance must remain separate, which
questions must remain open, and when those questions may be answered.

It is subordinate to the frozen Phase 1 contracts and the accepted prospective
post-R3 amendment. It may make their meaning more explicit for L03, but it cannot
change the hypothesis, common comparison groups, controls, metric relation
vocabulary, boundary ladder, terminal states, or claim ceiling. A conflict
fails closed and requires the controlling contract to reopen before
calibration.

The accepted drafting correction controls this brief's distance treatment:

```text
matched route structure
!= fixed induced geometry
!= matched functional coupling
!= matched causal/proper-time distance
!= experimentally demonstrated causal influence
```

This brief is intentionally not a result, source-admission record,
realization profile, field equation, metric selection, calibration record,
registration, or execution plan. In particular, it does not select:

- an edge-, node-, corridor-, or other field carrier;
- a native, producer-assisted, or missing-prerequisite realization;
- a deposition, reinforcement, decay, maintenance, saturation, or leakage law;
- a primary traversal response or distance surface;
- a topology instance, numeric value, seed, threshold, or attempt budget;
- an appendix activation; or
- an eventual rung or terminal class.

The purpose of freezing meaning before implementation is to prevent the first
convenient persistent counter, route score, or routing function from quietly
becoming the theory of a trail field.

Review R3 also constrains the later implementation process. P2-I3 may reuse
the common contract shape and compact evidence methods established by P2-I1
and P2-I2, but it should maintain one evidence-expandable checklist, one
cumulative lane decision record, compact identity projections, and one
lane-local control-resolution index before terminal closure.

```text

```

That relation does not establish L03. The ecology-specific discriminator is:

```text

-> route-local field formation
-> measured non-static field dynamics
-> changed later traversal through the field
```

```text
insufficient_repetition_case:
```

N29 and N30 may motivate trace-bearing surfaces, persistence, later
eligibility, pressure-loop candidates, replay methods, and missing producer or

L03 is therefore not “N30 repeated several times.” Its independent question
is whether repeated, costed activity forms a local field with observable
temporal behavior that changes the relation among later traversal
possibilities.

## 4. Central proposition

> A trail or stigmergic field is a non-private, route-local condition formed
> at cost through repeated activity, evolving through at least one measured
> non-static dynamic, and causally altering later traversal through the field.
> Registered topology, induced geometric distance, functional coupling,
> causal/proper-time distance, and experimentally demonstrated causal
> influence remain distinct observational surfaces. None is presumed to
> change merely because another changes.

A trail field is not reducible to:

- a persistent mark with no measured dynamic;
- an audit log or traversal-count table interpreted after execution;
- a source-addressed mailbox or private traverser memory;
- a route label or precomputed preference;
- a global route score or `argmax` selector;
- a controller-installed success flag;
- a direct symbolic message; or
- a later traversal difference with no field intervention chain.

“Trail” and “stigmergic” name the bounded ecological search direction. They do
not pre-assign pheromone, communication, coordination, learning, planning,
colony, or agency semantics.

The two interpretations remain distinct at closeout:

```text
trail_field_candidate:
  an evolving route-local field alters later traversal

stigmergic_field_candidate:
  one participant's attributable activity alters the field and thereby
  changes another eligible participant's later activity without direct
  addressing
```

These are bounded interpretation tags, not additional terminal classes or
rungs. A same-participant probe can support a trail-field reading but cannot
by itself support the stronger stigmergic mediation reading. A fresh
nondepositor probe may support that stronger reading only when participant
identity, field mediation, and direct-address exclusion are independently
resolved. Neither reading implies communication or coordination.

## 5. Exact causal question

P2-I3 asks:

> Can repeated costly activity make two routes with matched registered
> opportunities and matched baseline distance surfaces diverge in a
> traversal-relevant relation through an evolving route-local field, with
> withdrawal, clamp, relocation, and hidden-router controls showing that the
> effect follows the field?

Subordinate questions remain open until their declared decision points:

- Does repeated activity change the field beyond one quantity-matched pulse?
- Which non-static dynamics are expressed: reinforcement, decay, maintenance,
  saturation, leakage, or another controlled relation?
- Does the field remain after the depositor withdraws?
- Does it change without further deposition?
- Can recurring costly activity maintain it?
- Is additional deposition bounded or diminishing?
- Does later traversal follow the field when the field is relocated?
- Does current field state suffice, or is formation history load-bearing?
- Which registered distance or traversal surface changes?
- How long, through which carriers, and over what route-local extent does
  deposition remain causally relevant?

The question remains valid for positive, narrow, mixed, counter-directional,
null, blocked, or incomplete results. A result may expose generic trace
persistence, a constructed field, a proxy route score, an unsuitable native
surface, a missing field dynamic, or no reusable value without becoming an
L03 candidate.

## 6. Two-route causal kernel

### 6.1 Core topology

The smallest core capable of separating route structure from field dynamics
contains two alternative routes between the same origin and target:

```text
                 route A
              /-----------\
origin O ----<               >---- target T
              \-----------/
                 route B
```

Two routes permit field relocation, route-label exclusion, matched baseline
comparison, traversal redistribution, and dominant-field withdrawal. One
route may demonstrate persistence or decay but cannot by itself establish the
same spatial causal specificity.

The runtime must make later traversal encounter route-local carrier state.
The following form is inadmissible as the causal mechanism:

```text
preferred_route = argmax(global_trace_table)
```

An admissible realization must instead expose the registered local relation,
for example by making continuation at the branch depend on the local outgoing
route surfaces through native or explicitly bounded producer operations.

### 6.2 Baseline matching

Before field formation, the routes must match or explicitly account for:

- topology and route opportunities;
- hop counts and declared structural lengths;
- geometric distance surfaces;
- functional/coupling distance surfaces;
- causal/proper-time distance surfaces;
- capacity and admissibility;
- initial field state and history;
- traversal opportunity and response measurement; and
- participant support and cost context.

“Matched” means equal under the registered baseline contract or paired through
an explicit accounted contrast. It does not mean that every surface must
remain equal after activity.

Participant context is matched by declared role: depositor and later-probe
initial support, budget, access, admissibility, opportunity, and cost policy
must each match across routes and comparison arms except for the registered
intervention. The roles need not be the same participant identity.

### 6.3 Formation and probe

The candidate causal sequence is:

```text
matched baseline routes
    -> attributable costly activity on one route
    -> repeated route-local field formation
    -> at least one measured non-static dynamic
    -> depositor/probe episode and role separation
    -> fresh later traversal opportunity
    -> substrate-visible traversal consequence
    -> field-specific intervention and reconstruction
```

This separation is temporal and causal, not necessarily participant-identity
separation. The depositor and later traverser may be the same participant in
separate registered episodes or different participants. L03 does not require
several depositors as its ecological discriminator. Same-source,
multiple-source, source-label permutation, fresh nondepositor, and alternate
eligible traverser conditions are scope and transfer diagnostics. Different
participant identity becomes mandatory only for the stronger
`stigmergic_field_candidate` interpretation tag.

### 6.4 Post-formation observation

After formation, every registered surface is observed. No equality,
divergence, or direction is presumed. Legitimate patterns include:

```text
fixed topology + geometric deformation

fixed induced geometry + functional coupling change

fixed geometric and functional surfaces + live traversal change

causal-delay change without geometric shortening

combined changes across several surfaces

field dynamics without later traversal dependence
```

The final pattern is a valid lower observation but cannot satisfy the L03
ecology discriminator.

## 7. Field ontology

P2-I3 keeps the following objects distinct:

```text
route surface:
  one edge, corridor, or declared route-local carrier region

depositor activity:
  registered traversal or operation that may create a field change

deposition:
  attributable and costed transfer into the route-local field

field state:
  active runtime condition F_e(t) attached to route surface e

field history:
  deposition, reinforcement, decay, maintenance, saturation, leakage, and
  intervention lineage relevant to continuation

later traverser:
  a fresh eligible participant encountering the route field

traversal response:
  substrate-visible route or continuation consequence

audit lineage:
  who acted, where, when, through which operation, and with what cost

causal field:
  the route-local state or history actually encountered by later traversal
```

Initial realization vocabulary is restricted to `route node`, `route edge`,
`corridor`, `field carrier`, `landmark`, `origin`, and `target`. These objects
must not be called RC identity basins merely because the distance theory uses
basin graphs. Identity-basin language requires a demonstrated mapping to the
relevant support and connectivity definition.

The causal field may live on an edge, node, corridor segment, or constructed
route surface. That location is selected only after source audit. A producer
that stores the final route decision rather than an independently
intervenable field is inadmissible.

## 8. Temporal field dynamics

### 8.1 General relation

The brief freezes dynamic meanings, not an equation. A candidate family may
have a relation of the form:

```text
F_e(t + 1) = update(
  F_e(t),
  deposition_e(t),
  reinforcement,
  decay,
  maintenance,
  saturation,
  leakage,
  interventions
)
```

The exact update law, units, schedule, and parameters belong to realization
and registration. They cannot be selected after observing candidate outcomes.

### 8.2 Required meanings

- **Deposition:** valid activity creates a route-local field change.
- **Reinforcement:** later valid activity changes the field relation relative
  to an earlier deposit.
- **Decay:** the field changes after depositor activity stops.
- **Maintenance:** new costly activity offsets decay or preserves a relation.
- **Saturation:** additional activity produces a bounded or diminishing field
  change.
- **Leakage:** field influence or capacity escapes the declared carrier, when
  applicable.

For minimum L03 support, repeated costly formation and at least one measured
non-static dynamic are mandatory. “Repeated” has a logical floor of at least
two temporally distinguishable attributable formation events; the exact finite
count, timing, and amount remain registration decisions. A single pulse cannot
satisfy repetition regardless of its quantity. The selected dynamic may be
reinforcement, decay, maintenance, or saturation. That selected dynamic must
either change later traversal directly or change a mediator whose independent
intervention changes later traversal. Co-occurring dynamics with no causal
path to the registered traversal relation cannot carry L03 support.
Persistence alone remains below the L03 boundary.

### 8.3 Current state and formation history

Equal current field states may have equal or different future trajectories.
P2-I3 must retain enough history to test rather than assume current-state
sufficiency:

```text
same current F(t*) + different formation history
  -> compare later F and traversal trajectories
```

“Same current field” means equality of every registered continuation-relevant
current field component, not equality of one projected trace scalar. Depending
on the realization, the equality contract may include:

- all route-local field values;
- pending decay state and update phase;
- maintenance phase;
- saturation, reserve, or capacity state;
- leakage state;
- producer cursor or other continuation state;
- pending scheduled field updates;
- route-local support relevant to the field; and
- current inputs to every registered distance policy.

Anything omitted from this equality contract must be demonstrated irrelevant
to continuation. Otherwise the comparison is not state-matched and cannot
support a formation-history interpretation.

This is a bounded discriminator, not a repetition of the complete P2-I2
state/history/hybrid program. The proportional default is one selected field
realization plus one complete-state-matched formation-history discriminator.
Multiple full realization modes require source-audit and causal-design
evidence that one realization cannot answer the state/history question without
changing semantics, plus an explicit owner decision. If history is
load-bearing, the relevant state must be independently intervenable and
included in restoration. If it is not, the result may still support a
current-state-carried trail field.

### 8.4 False-trace interpretation

A false trace is installed through the declared field interface without the
registered depositor history. Its result is not predetermined:

- if later traversal follows it, current field state is causally sufficient
  at that probe boundary, but false installation does not satisfy costly
  formation;
- if later traversal does not follow it while a matched formed field does,
  formation history or another continuation state may be load-bearing; and
- if installation bypasses the declared field interface or directly writes
  the outcome, the control is invalid.

I03 must define the interface family and I06 must freeze its exact operation
and carrier binding before any false-trace or candidate execution. The false
trace may intervene directly on the carrier, but it must not use an
outcome-writing path, and the interface cannot change after execution begins.

## 9. Registered structure and five observation surfaces

### 9.1 Separation contract

P2-I3 retains five parallel surfaces:

| Surface | Experimental role | Claim restriction |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Registered structure | Topology, hops, route opportunities, declared lengths, capacities, and availability | Structural equality does not prove induced geometric equality |
| Geometric distance | State-derived edge lengths and accumulated or shortest geometric paths | A geometric change is not automatically faster traversal |
| Functional distance | Conductance- or flux-coupling separation and functional shortest paths | Stronger coupling is not automatically causal influence |
| Causal/proper-time distance | Edge delay, accumulated route delay, proper time, and possible arrival | Annotation must remain separate from live timing |
| Experimental causal influence | Intervention-established deposition-to-field-to-traversal relation | Requires executed controls and reconstruction, not annotation alone |

No scalar, rank, or preferred-route label may collapse these surfaces.

Later observation and checklist contracts must assign every distance surface
one fail-closed semantic disposition:

```text
measured
derived_annotation_only
not_applicable_with_rationale
unavailable_missing_surface
```

Those semantic dispositions must be projected through existing machine
vocabulary or an explicitly reviewed pre-freeze extension; the brief does not
silently add schema values. A realization must not invent a geometric,
functional, or causal surface merely to fill a panel. Experimental causal
influence, however, must be measured and reconstructed for a positive L03
result. It cannot be annotation-only or not applicable.

### 9.2 Registered-structure panel

Retain:

- node and edge identities;
- topology and route availability;
- hop count and declared structural lengths;
- capacities and admissibility;
- origin, target, branch, and carrier roles; and
- any topology or availability intervention.

Topology change and metric deformation must be reported separately.

### 9.3 Geometric-distance panel

Retain, when available:

- route-local geometric edge lengths;
- accumulated route length;
- shortest induced geometric path;
- the policy and state used to derive the length; and
- the time trajectory of geometric distance.

Do not call topology, hop count, or chart position an induced RC distance
without the registered derivation.

### 9.4 Functional-distance panel

Retain, when available:

- base conductance and route-local coupling;
- flux coupling and directionality;
- inverse-coupling path separation;
- functional shortest path or coupling hierarchy; and
- the time trajectory and policy used.

The direction of a field effect is not fixed. A trail may make traversal
functionally closer, farther, gated, or unstable. The experiment must observe
the relation rather than optimize toward “shorter.”

### 9.5 Causal/proper-time panel

Retain, when available:

- edge causal delay;
- accumulated route delay;
- node proper-time state and last-update state;
- annotation-only versus semi-causal versus live-runtime status;
- earliest possible and observed arrival; and
- the policy connecting geometry, flux, delay, and traversal.

An annotation-only distance or cone remains derived evidence. It cannot be
reported as executed traversal or experimental causality.

### 9.6 Experimental-causal panel

Retain:

- deposition-to-field lineage;
- field-to-traversal lineage;
- withdrawal, clamp, shuffle or relocation, and matched-diversion outcomes;
- effect lag and persistence horizon;
- route-local extent or carrier chain;
- hidden-router and participant-label exclusions;
- fresh-probe identity; and
- replay, restoration, and reconstruction results.

This panel establishes the L03 causal claim. The other panels describe where
and how the controlled relation is expressed.

### 9.7 Distance trajectories and signatures

The L03 object is time-indexed:

```text
t -> registered structure
t -> geometric distance
t -> functional distance
t -> causal/proper-time distance
t -> experimental field and traversal relation
```

Useful preregistered signatures may include:

```text
fixed topology, changed geometry

fixed geometry, changed functional or causal distance

equal present field, different future field or traversal trajectory

equal field magnitude, different carrier continuity

field relocation, corresponding traversal-effect relocation
```

These are classification signatures, not required directions.

## 10. Episode architecture

P2-I3 requires a longitudinal trajectory rather than one endpoint probe. A
candidate registration should distinguish these semantic phases:

1. **Baseline:** matched routes, reference field, and fresh-probe response.
2. **First deposition:** one attributable costly activity and its field
   consequence.
3. **Repeated formation:** additional matched activities and the selected
   non-static dynamic.
4. **Fresh probe:** a later traverser encounters the registered field.
5. **Withdrawal:** deposition stops while field evolution remains observable.
6. **Delayed probes:** fresh probes at frozen comparable times.
7. **Maintenance contrast:** periodic costly deposits under a separate arm.
8. **Saturation diagnostic:** continued deposits under a bounded separate arm.

The phases may be independent branches from registered checkpoints rather
than one adaptive run. Comparable causal time, initial state, attempt
accounting, and branch identity must be preserved. Strong and highest-level
diagnostics cannot be inserted into the minimum candidate after seeing its
outcome.

Calibration preregistration must choose an observation cadence capable of
resolving the selected dynamic, and registration must freeze the exact cadence
relative to its declared timescale. No universal samples-per-half-life value
is assumed before the realization and dynamic law are known.

## 11. Cost and field accounting

The experiment keeps four accounting roles distinct:

```text
traversal cost
deposition cost
maintenance cost
field reserve or capacity
```

For each field-forming operation, later registration should retain:

- participant budget before and after;
- traversal amount and cost;
- deposition amount and cost;
- field before and after;
- maintenance or leakage where applicable;
- operation and carrier receipts; and
- budget, source, and producer provenance.

An activity-without-deposition control preserves traversal activity, timing,
opportunity, and non-field costs while removing only the field-producing
transition. A free controller write, unrecorded reserve, or field change with
no declared cost cannot satisfy costly attributable formation.

“Field economy” may be used only for this bounded accounting relation. It does
not imply resource economy, cooperation, collective investment, colony
regulation, or metabolism.

## 12. Core comparison and causal-control structure

### 12.1 Logical comparison cells

The exact finite matrix is deferred, but the semantic design must be able to
represent:

```text
reference
single_trace_or_first_deposition
repeated_deposition_candidate
quantity_matched_pulse
activity_with_deposition_disabled
withdrawal_and_decay
maintenance_contrast
saturation_diagnostic
trace_shuffle_or_relocation
false_trace
field_clamp
route_label_permutation
hidden_router_exclusion
transfer_or_contrast
```

These are logical roles, not an authorization to execute. Calibration and
registration must decide which strong or highest-level diagnostics are
separate branches, which frozen common groups they realize, and which are
validly inapplicable.

### 12.2 Essential comparisons

**Dynamic field versus static trace:** distinguish an evolving field from one
persistent installed mark.

**Repeated deposition versus quantity-matched pulse:** preserve total
deposition while varying temporal formation. Equality is informative and may
indicate current quantity rather than repeated temporal structure is
load-bearing.

This is distinct from state matching:

```text
quantity_matched:
  total deposited quantity is equal
  complete current field at the probe may differ

state_matched:
  every registered continuation-relevant current field component is equal
  formation history differs
```

The two contrasts require separate operational-hypothesis and comparison-cell
identities and separate interpretation rules. A quantity-matched pulse cannot
establish history dependence unless the complete current causal field is also
state-matched.

**Activity with versus without field writing:** preserve nearby movement,
timing, opportunity, and cost context while withdrawing deposition.

**Maintained versus unmaintained field:** begin from comparable field state and
vary subsequent costly activity.

**Field relocation:** use mode-aware semantics without changing route labels
or a global selector:

```text
current_state_relocation:
  move the complete registered current field state from route A to route B

active_history_relocation:
  move or reconstruct both complete current field state and
  continuation-relevant active history on route B
```

Relocation acts on the registered carrier state. Route names, route labels,
participant labels, and precomputed preference values are excluded from the
relocated payload and are verified unchanged.

Active-history relocation is valid only when history is registered causal
state that is restorable and independently intervenable. Copying audit lineage
does not make history causal. Where that boundary is unavailable, P2-I3 uses
current-state relocation and classifies active-history relocation as
inapplicable, unavailable, or separately constructed.

**Current field versus formation history:** compare matched present field
state under different valid histories without importing the entire L02
program.

### 12.3 Lane-specific controls

The frozen lane controls retain their exact IDs and meanings:

- `AE01-L03-CTRL-01`: deposition withdrawal;
- `AE01-L03-CTRL-02`: trace shuffle;
- `AE01-L03-CTRL-03`: false-trace injection;
- `AE01-L03-CTRL-04`: decay/reinforcement manipulation; and
- `AE01-L03-CTRL-05`: fixed-route or hidden-router exclusion.

Every common control receives an explicit applicability disposition. All five
frozen L03 controls receive retained terminal outcomes. A realization-specific
`not_applicable` disposition is valid only when the controlling contract
permits it and its rationale does not erase a mandatory support signature;
otherwise the control must execute before terminal closure. A valid result
contrary to the expected direction is scientific information; a missing or
invalid mandatory control makes the affected claim incomplete.

### 12.4 Hidden-router boundary

The implementation must exclude:

- global route ranking;
- source-addressed route selection;
- traversal-count lookup used as a final preference;
- controller-computed success or preferred-route fields;
- route labels used as causal state; and
- a producer that reads the complete future matrix or candidate identity.

A route-arbitration surface is not automatically disallowed, but its inputs,
scope, causal locality, and runtime role must be audited. If the arbitration
mechanism computes the conclusion rather than exposing traversal to the
field, it cannot satisfy L03.

## 13. Minimum, strong, and highest results within L03

### 13.1 Minimum L03 support

Minimum support requires all of:

```text
at least two temporally distinguishable attributable formation events
+ nonzero declared formation cost
+ at least one measured non-static field dynamic
```
