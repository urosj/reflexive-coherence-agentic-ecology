# AE01 Lane Hypotheses

**Status:** revised and frozen at P1-I4 revision 0.24

**Hypothesis-set version:** `1.1.0`

**Evidence effect:** no lane executed and no result assigned

## 1. Shared reading rule

Each hypothesis asks whether a bounded ecology-side relation can be supported,
partially exposed, rejected, or converted into a precise missing-surface
demand. The terminal meanings and attempt limits come from the
[outcome and stopping contract](outcome-and-stopping-contract.md). Common
controls and failures come from the
[control and failure register](control-and-failure-register.md). Threshold
relations, lane boundary ladders, two-axis readings, and next moves come from
the [developmental interpretation contract](developmental-interpretation-contract.md).

The signatures below are logical requirements. P1-I5 must freeze metric
definitions, thresholds, deterministic seeds, variants, attempts, runtime
profiles, and reconstruction commands before execution. Those implementation
choices may operationalize a signature but may not weaken or replace it.

## 2. `AE01-H-L01` — Minimal shared-medium niche formation

**Lane:** `AE01-L01`

**Hypothesis:** an attributable perturbation of a declared non-private medium
can leave a bounded persistent surface condition that changes later formation,
persistence, re-entry, cost, or susceptibility of a locally differentiated
participant within a declared support context.

`Niche` is the ecology interpretation of the functional conditioning relation.
It is not an inherited N30 label, a population-ecology claim, or evidence of an
organism or ecology regime.

### Required comparison cells

- matched reference with participant, medium, and support context declared;
- candidate perturbation followed by a later formation/re-entry opportunity;
- medium freeze or candidate-trace withdrawal;
- trace shuffle or source-lineage substitution;
- parent-context removal or held-constant contrast;
- susceptibility or support inversion; and
- one frozen carrier, timescale, or participant contrast.

### Support signatures

All must hold:

1. A participant carrier and medium carrier are separately identified or have
   a non-circular co-constitution account.
2. The perturbation produces an attributable, auditable medium-surface change.
3. The surface change persists or decays through a declared bounded window.
4. A later formation, persistence, re-entry, cost, or susceptibility measure
   depends on that changed surface under intervention.
5. Withdrawal, shuffle, source, parent-context, budget, and semantic controls
   block direct-message, label-only, free-trace, and passive-environment paths.

### Outcome signatures

- `supported_bounded_candidate`: all support signatures pass within the lane
  ceiling.
- `partial_or_mixed`: conditioning is visible but unstable, producer-carried,
  fixture-locked, or ambiguous in parent context (`AE01-F02`, `AE01-F03`,
  `AE01-F06`, or `AE01-F10`).
- `not_supported`: a valid completed probe shows no attributable surface
  conditioning or later dependence (`AE01-F01`, `AE01-F04`, or `AE01-F05`).
- `blocked_missing_prerequisite`: participant formation, susceptibility,
  medium persistence, or the required discriminator cannot be instantiated.
- `incomplete_execution`: one or more mandatory cells cannot complete or
  reconstruct within the frozen attempt budget.

### Mandatory controls and stopping

All common controls apply when relevant, with an explicit inapplicability
rationale otherwise. Controls `AE01-CTRL-01`, `02`, `04` through `06`, `08`
through `12`, `14` through `17`, and `19` are especially direct here.
Lane-specific controls are:

- `AE01-L01-CTRL-01`: medium freeze while preserving participant opportunity;
- `AE01-L01-CTRL-02`: trace shuffle with matched trace quantity;
- `AE01-L01-CTRL-03`: susceptibility inversion;
- `AE01-L01-CTRL-04`: parent-context separation; and
- `AE01-L01-CTRL-05`: constructed-scaffold withdrawal when applicable.

Stop when the complete frozen matrix yields one terminal classification. No
extra variants may be added to rescue a weak niche interpretation.

**Maximum claim:** bounded niche-conditioning demand pattern.

## 3. `AE01-H-L02` — Shared-pool co-conditioning

**Lane:** `AE01-L02`

**Hypothesis:** distinguishable perturbations from at least two source events
can alter one auditable non-private pool so that later continuation depends on
the combined shared surface history rather than private partitions, direct
messages, or a global controller.

Co-conditioning does not require semantic collaboration or a non-linear
interaction. It requires multiple attributable contributions to one functional
carrier and a later response that depends on the resulting shared state.

### Required comparison cells

- reference pool with access scope, reserve, leakage, and maintenance declared;
- each source contribution separately;
- combined contributions in at least two frozen orders where order can matter;
- pooled-state shuffle preserving marginal contribution quantity;
- one contributor removed or source attribution permuted;
- direct-address/global-controller exclusion; and
- one capacity, contributor, or access-scope contrast.

### Support signatures

All must hold:

1. Contributions enter one declared carrier accessible beyond a private
   sender-receiver path.
2. Source lineage remains attributable without partitioning the pool into
   disguised mailboxes.
3. The later response depends on combined pool state or history, not merely on
   contributor labels or scheduler state.
4. Reserve, depletion, leakage, and maintenance are measured or explicitly
   declared inapplicable with a valid rationale.
5. Single-source, shuffle, attribution, producer, and global-state controls
   preserve the claimed shared-pool distinction.

### Outcome signatures

- `supported_bounded_candidate`: a functional shared pool and multi-source
  conditioning relation pass all controls.
- `partial_or_mixed`: a shared surface is visible but combined-history
  dependence is weak, unstable, producer-carried, or fixture-locked.
- `not_supported`: the apparent pool is private/global storage, has no later
  functional dependence, or no distinguishable co-conditioning occurs.
- `blocked_missing_prerequisite`: shared access, source lineage, or combined
  state cannot be represented without an undeclared producer.
- `incomplete_execution`: the frozen source/order/control matrix cannot
  complete or reconstruct.

### Mandatory controls and stopping

All common controls apply when relevant, with controls `AE01-CTRL-01` through
`09`, `11`, `12`, and `14` through `19` especially direct here. Lane-specific
controls are:

- `AE01-L02-CTRL-01`: each contributor alone;
- `AE01-L02-CTRL-02`: contributor deletion and label permutation;
- `AE01-L02-CTRL-03`: pooled-history shuffle;
- `AE01-L02-CTRL-04`: direct-address/global-state substitution; and
- `AE01-L02-CTRL-05`: pool freeze with contributor activity preserved.

Stop after all frozen source, order, and contrast cells classify the pool as
functional, partial, absent, blocked, or incomplete.

**Maximum claim:** bounded shared-pool co-conditioning demand pattern.

## 4. `AE01-H-L03` — Trail or stigmergic field

**Lane:** `AE01-L03`

**Hypothesis:** costly deposition on a declared non-private surface can leave a
trace with measurable persistence, decay, reinforcement, or saturation that
changes later route or continuation eligibility without symbolic-message or
pheromone semantics.

### Required comparison cells

- reference route/continuation surface with no candidate trace;
- attributable deposition followed by a later traversal opportunity;
- deposition disabled with movement/activity preserved;
- spatial or carrier trace shuffle;
- false-trace injection;
- decay and reinforcement manipulation; and
- one geometry, timescale, or susceptibility contrast.

### Support signatures

All must hold:

1. Deposition has an explicit source, carrier, timing, and nonzero declared
   cost or budget effect.
2. The surface changes independently of a private message or hidden routing
   table.
3. Persistence/decay and any reinforcement/saturation behavior are measured.
4. Later route or continuation eligibility changes because of the surface
   trace under shuffle, false-trace, and deposition-withdrawal controls.
5. No semantic message, coordination, learning, or ant/colony ecology label is
   used as a result.

### Outcome signatures

- `supported_bounded_candidate`: the full deposition-trace-later-response chain
  passes.
- `partial_or_mixed`: a trace is visible but unstable, producer-carried,
  fixture-locked, or its later effect is ambiguous.
- `not_supported`: there is no functional trace or route effect, or success is
  only a proxy/global-routing artifact.
- `blocked_missing_prerequisite`: surface deposition, trace dynamics, or later
  routing susceptibility cannot be represented.
- `incomplete_execution`: a required trace/control cell or reconstruction does
  not complete.

### Mandatory controls and stopping

All common controls apply when relevant, with controls `AE01-CTRL-01`, `02`,
`04` through `06`, `08`, `09`, `11`, `12`, and `14` through `19` especially
direct here. Lane-specific controls are:

- `AE01-L03-CTRL-01`: deposition withdrawal;
- `AE01-L03-CTRL-02`: trace shuffle;
- `AE01-L03-CTRL-03`: false-trace injection;
- `AE01-L03-CTRL-04`: decay/reinforcement manipulation; and
- `AE01-L03-CTRL-05`: fixed-route or hidden-router exclusion.

Stop after the frozen trace dynamics and later-response comparisons yield one
terminal classification; additional trace tuning requires revision and rerun.

**Maximum claim:** bounded trail or stigmergic demand pattern.

## 5. `AE01-H-L04` — Nursery or support field

**Lane:** `AE01-L04`

**Hypothesis:** earlier generative or redistributive activity can alter a
declared shared support surface so that later formation or persistence of a
fragile local participant changes as a function of that surface and its cost,
without importing care, altruism, cooperation, reproduction, or generative
agency semantics.

### Required comparison cells

- fragile-participant reference under declared support and susceptibility;
- prior support-generating or redistributive activity;
- support freeze or withdrawal with nearby activity preserved;
- matched neutral/extractive redistribution contrast;
- susceptibility inversion or robust-participant contrast;
- hidden producer and direct allocation exclusion; and
- one support level, timescale, or participant-carrier contrast.

### Support signatures

All must hold:

1. The support surface is an auditable carrier rather than a producer story or
   direct allocation command.
2. Earlier activity changes support availability, eligibility, cost, or
   susceptibility through that surface.
3. Later fragile formation or persistence depends on the changed support
   surface relative to neutral/extractive and withdrawal controls.
4. Support production, depletion, leakage, and maintenance are accounted for.
5. Semantic care/cooperation/reproduction claims and native-support claims
   remain blocked.

### Outcome signatures

- `supported_bounded_candidate`: a bounded support-conditioned admissibility
  relation passes all controls.
- `partial_or_mixed`: support dependence is unstable, producer-carried,
  fixture-locked, or susceptibility-specific beyond the declared boundary.
- `not_supported`: later formation is independent of the support surface, or
  apparent support is direct allocation/proxy success.
- `blocked_missing_prerequisite`: fragile formation, susceptibility, support
  accounting, or producer withdrawal cannot be instantiated.
- `incomplete_execution`: mandatory support/contrast cells cannot complete or
  reconstruct.

### Mandatory controls and stopping

All common controls apply when relevant, with controls `AE01-CTRL-01` through
`12` and `14` through `19` especially direct here. Lane-specific controls are:

- `AE01-L04-CTRL-01`: support-surface freeze/withdrawal;
- `AE01-L04-CTRL-02`: neutral and extractive redistribution contrasts;
- `AE01-L04-CTRL-03`: fragile-versus-robust susceptibility contrast;
- `AE01-L04-CTRL-04`: hidden direct-allocation exclusion; and
- `AE01-L04-CTRL-05`: support budget and leakage audit.

Stop when the finite support, susceptibility, and withdrawal matrix closes.
Failure to naturalize a constructed support producer remains visible debt.

**Maximum claim:** bounded support-conditioned admissibility demand pattern.

## 6. `AE01-H-L05` — Boundary-conditioned exchange

**Lane:** `AE01-L05`

**Hypothesis:** a maintained selective-coupling surface can condition ingress,
egress, transformation, permeability, leakage, or repair between declared
sides, with later closure consequences that depend on boundary state rather
than a hard-coded transfer route.

### Required comparison cells

- reference sides, carriers, parent context, and maintained boundary state;
- candidate exchange through the boundary;
- boundary removal or permeability neutralization;
- selectivity inversion or shuffled boundary state;
- direct-transfer bypass exclusion;
- maintenance/repair withdrawal and leakage audit; and
- one carrier, permeability, or load contrast.

### Support signatures

All must hold:

1. Boundary state is auditable and distinct from labels for inside/outside.
2. Exchange lineage identifies ingress, egress, transformation, and leakage as
   applicable.
3. Exchange or later closure changes under boundary-state intervention.
4. Selectivity, maintenance, repair, and leakage have declared costs/budgets.
5. Direct bypass, hard-coded routing, organism/membrane semantics, and native
   multi-basin claims remain blocked.

### Outcome signatures

- `supported_bounded_candidate`: boundary state functionally conditions the
  bounded exchange relation.
- `partial_or_mixed`: exchange is selective but unstable, producer-carried,
  fixture-locked, or repair/leakage remains unresolved.
- `not_supported`: boundary state has no causal role or apparent exchange uses
  direct routing/proxy labels.
- `blocked_missing_prerequisite`: selective coupling, side identity, lineage,
  or maintenance cannot be represented.
- `incomplete_execution`: a required exchange/control cell or reconstruction
  does not complete.

### Mandatory controls and stopping

All common controls apply when relevant, with controls `AE01-CTRL-01` through
`12` and `14` through `19` especially direct here. Lane-specific controls are:

- `AE01-L05-CTRL-01`: boundary removal/permeability neutralization;
- `AE01-L05-CTRL-02`: selectivity inversion;
- `AE01-L05-CTRL-03`: direct-transfer bypass exclusion;
- `AE01-L05-CTRL-04`: maintenance/repair withdrawal; and
- `AE01-L05-CTRL-05`: leakage and lineage audit.

Stop after all boundary states and controls close; inability to define the
sides or parent context is blocked, not negative evidence.

**Maximum claim:** bounded boundary-conditioned exchange demand pattern.

## 7. `AE01-H-L06` — Capacity circulation

**Lane:** `AE01-L06`

**Hypothesis:** auditable capacity can move through declared carriers, deplete,
replenish, leak, and maintain floors so that later eligibility changes depend
on circulation history without semantic resource exchange, cooperation,
exploitation, metabolism, or hidden free transfer.

### Required comparison cells

- reference capacity ledger, carriers, floors, and leakage policy;
- candidate circulation across at least two declared legs;
- circulation disabled with local activity preserved;
- ledger/source-lineage shuffle;
- free-capacity injection and hidden-transfer exclusion;
- depletion/replenishment and floor stress; and
- one topology, capacity, or timescale contrast.

### Support signatures

All must hold:

1. Capacity identity and lineage are auditable across every circulation leg.
2. Depletion, replenishment, leakage, maintenance, and floors are explicit.
3. Later eligibility or continuation depends on circulation history under
   disable, shuffle, and injection controls.
4. The declared budget closes within its frozen accounting tolerance; missing
   capacity cannot be supplied by an unrecorded producer.
5. Semantic resource-economy and native ecology-circulation labels remain
   blocked.

### Outcome signatures

- `supported_bounded_candidate`: the complete circulation-history and later
  eligibility relation passes.
- `partial_or_mixed`: some legs work but lineage, stability, transfer, leakage,
  or combined operation fails.
- `not_supported`: capacity does not circulate functionally, later eligibility
  is independent, or success is global accounting/proxy state.
- `blocked_missing_prerequisite`: multi-leg handoff, source lineage, floors, or
  leakage accounting cannot be represented.
- `incomplete_execution`: stress/control cells or reconstruction do not
  complete within budget.

### Mandatory controls and stopping

All common controls apply when relevant, with controls `AE01-CTRL-01` through
`09`, `11`, `12`, and `14` through `19` especially direct here. Lane-specific
controls are:

- `AE01-L06-CTRL-01`: circulation disablement;
- `AE01-L06-CTRL-02`: capacity-ledger/source shuffle;
- `AE01-L06-CTRL-03`: free-capacity injection detection;
- `AE01-L06-CTRL-04`: depletion/replenishment floor stress; and
- `AE01-L06-CTRL-05`: multi-leg leakage and conservation audit.

Stop after the fixed topology, stress, and contrast matrix closes. Component
success never substitutes for complete circulation.

**Maximum claim:** bounded capacity-circulation demand pattern.

## 8. `AE01-H-L07` — Parent-basin modulation demand and missing surface

**Lane:** `AE01-L07`

**Hypothesis:** the admitted source-current graph surface can be classified as
adequate, unsuitable, or missing for a parent-basin state to modulate separable
local susceptibilities without central command; when it is missing or
unsuitable, a minimal constructed ecology contrast can expose a precise future
graph-side discriminator without counting as M3/M4 or native evidence.

This is principally a demand-classification hypothesis. Its supported result
is a bounded classification and discriminator, not proof of parent-basin
modulation.

### Required comparison cells

- source-current artifact inspection of candidate parent/local surfaces;
- declared parent basin and at least two separable local susceptibilities;
- parent-state variation with local state held or matched;
- parent-basin separation or label-only substitution;
- global-parameter/central-scheduler exclusion;
- local susceptibility inversion; and
- constructed-scaffold withdrawal when a construction is necessary.

### Support signatures

All must hold:

1. Existing graph surfaces are classified as apparently adequate, unsuitable,
   or missing with exact source references and claim ceilings.
2. The required parent/local relation, state handoff, and counterfactual are
   operationally defined.
3. A proposed discriminator distinguishes parent-conditioned local response
   from duplicated local parameters, a central scheduler, or post-hoc labels.
4. Any constructed contrast declares necessity, minimality, withdrawal, debt,
   and realization profile and remains constructed.
5. Positive M3/M4, shared-medium coordination, colony agency, and ecology
   regime claims remain blocked.

### Outcome signatures

- `supported_bounded_candidate`: a complete adequate/unsuitable/missing-surface
  classification and bounded discriminator is supported. This does not mean
  the parent-modulation mechanism is supported.
- `partial_or_mixed`: the source tension is visible but the discriminator,
  separability, or construction withdrawal remains ambiguous.
- `not_supported`: the proposed demand is unnecessary or contradicted because
  an existing adequate surface and bounded probe path are identified; this is
  not positive M3/M4 evidence.
- `blocked_missing_prerequisite`: source-current fields are insufficient even
  to classify the demand, with the missing information recorded.
- `incomplete_execution`: requested inspection, live contrast, or
  reconstruction cannot complete.

### Mandatory controls and stopping

All common controls apply, especially `AE01-CTRL-02`, `10`, `13` through `17`,
and `19`. Lane-specific controls are:

- `AE01-L07-CTRL-01`: parent-basin label-only substitution;
- `AE01-L07-CTRL-02`: global-parameter/central-scheduler exclusion;
- `AE01-L07-CTRL-03`: parent/local separation and matched-local contrast;
- `AE01-L07-CTRL-04`: local susceptibility inversion; and
- `AE01-L07-CTRL-05`: constructed-scaffold withdrawal.

Stop once the existing surface is classified and the future discriminator or
missing-information contract is complete. Do not keep the lane open waiting
for a graph-side implementation outside AE01.

**Maximum claim:** bounded parent-basin modulation demand and missing-surface
classification; positive M3/M4 evidence is not expected.

## 9. Shared lane claim boundary

No lane hypothesis authorizes another lane's conclusion, cross-lane
recurrence, successful composition, catalog admission, domain promotion, or
N31+ selection. Those require their dedicated synthesis and closeout records.
