# Post-R3 Future-Lane Ecology-Discriminator Amendment

**Status:** accepted prospective amendment for unexecuted lanes

**Effective date:** 2026-07-12

**Applies to:** `AE01-L02` through `AE01-L07` before their lane-local theory,
calibration, registration, or candidate-execution gates

**Does not alter:** closed `AE01-L01`, the frozen P2-I1 hypothesis identity,
retained P2-I1 evidence, shared schemas, stable lane IDs, or existing display
names

## 1. Purpose

N29 and N30 already established or motivated several graph-side relations used
by AE01: non-private medium state, attributable traces, later eligibility,
generative/extractive and circulatory candidates, boundary surfaces, and
explicit parent-modulation gaps. A future AE01 lane must not receive its
ecology-side terminal classification merely by reproducing one of those
relations under ecological vocabulary.

Every future lane must therefore bind three distinct objects before
calibration:

```text
inherited_graph_relation
ecology_specific_discriminator
controlled_ecology_consequence
```

A `supported_bounded_candidate` requires the ecology-specific discriminator.
Reproducing only the inherited relation remains a valid lower-level
observation at its original evidence role and claim ceiling, but cannot satisfy
the lane. A valid completed probe with no ecology discriminator closes as
`not_supported`; inability to represent the discriminator closes as
`blocked_missing_prerequisite`. An unstable or partly expressed discriminator
may close as `partial_or_mixed` under the existing hypothesis and failure
vocabularies.

This amendment narrows future positive interpretation. It supplies no evidence
and does not force a positive result.

## 3. Lane requirements

### 3.1 `AE01-L02` — shared-pool co-conditioning

The existing hypothesis already requires at least two attributable
contributions, single-source cells, combined states, order contrasts,
contributor deletion, and global/private exclusions. Its contract meaning is
clarified, not substantively changed:

- pool support requires later dependence on combined shared state or history;
- co-occurrence, sequential private writes, and a global sum do not suffice;
- non-linearity is optional, but joint functional dependence is mandatory.

### 3.2 `AE01-L03` — trail or stigmergic field

This is a binding targeted amendment. Static trace persistence plus later
eligibility is already within N30's relation and cannot satisfy L03.

Before `P2-I3-CAL-PRE-GATE`, the lane must preregister:

- nonzero deposition or maintenance cost;
- at least two trace-history conditions involving repeated activity or an
  equivalent controlled history contrast;
- at least one measured dynamic beyond static persistence: decay,
  reinforcement, saturation, or active maintenance;
- a later traversal or continuation distribution that depends on that dynamic;
- deposition withdrawal, trace shuffle, false-trace, and hidden-router
  controls; and
- a stopping rule that permits an inherited trace-only result to close below
  the L03 boundary.

The display name remains “Trail or stigmergic field.” Symbolic pheromone,
message, learning, coordination, colony, and ecology claims remain blocked.

### 3.3 `AE01-L04` — nursery or support field

The existing fragile-versus-robust and support-accounting requirements control
the ecology distinction. The future brief must foreground them:

- the same or comparable support history must have different consequences for
  matched fragile and robust formation or persistence;
- production, depletion, leakage, and maintenance must be measured or validly
  classified as inapplicable; and
- generic improvement or direct allocation cannot satisfy the lane.

No care, altruism, cooperation, reproduction, or generative-agency meaning is
added.

### 3.4 `AE01-L05` — boundary-conditioned exchange

The existing hypothesis already distinguishes a maintained selective interface
from transport. The future brief must name maintenance as the ecological
discriminator and bind:

- declared sides and boundary state;
- selective ingress/egress or transformation;
- maintenance or repair cost;
- leakage and later closure consequence; and
- boundary removal plus direct-transfer bypass controls.

A fixed route or successful transfer without boundary-state dependence remains
below the lane.

### 3.5 `AE01-L06` — capacity circulation

This is a binding targeted amendment. Two or more one-way transport legs do not
by themselves constitute circulation.

Before `P2-I6-CAL-PRE-GATE`, the lane must preregister:

- a cycle with at least two declared legs and an explicit capacity identity;
- depletion at a source or intermediate carrier;
- replenishment, return, or another budget-closing re-entry leg;
- participant or carrier floors whose later condition depends on the cycle;
- leakage and conservation accounting over the complete cycle;
- disablement, source/ledger shuffle, free-injection, and floor-stress
  controls; and
- a stopping rule that classifies linear transport as insufficient even when
  every individual handoff succeeds.

The cycle need not imply metabolism, resource economy, cooperation, or
exploitation. Those semantics remain blocked.

### 3.6 `AE01-L07` — parent-basin modulation demand

L07 remains a demand-classification lane. It must distinguish:

- a parent-conditioned change in matched separable local susceptibilities;
- duplicated local parameters;
- a central scheduler or global variable; and
- a genuinely missing or unsuitable source-current surface.

The expected positive result is a bounded adequate/unsuitable/missing-surface
classification and future discriminator, not M3/M4 evidence.

## 4. Future lane-brief and gate projection

Every future lane brief must contain a section with these fields:

```text
inherited_graph_relation
ecology_specific_discriminator
controlled_ecology_consequence
insufficient_repetition_case
discriminator_controls
claim_ceiling
```

Before calibration, the lane checklist must confirm that:

1. its plain-language question foregrounds the ecology discriminator;
2. the candidate and control matrix can falsify that discriminator;
3. a reproduction of the inherited relation cannot reach the lane's supported
   terminal class;
4. its machine policy and compact control-resolution index bind the same
   distinction; and
5. no earlier lane conclusion is consumed as evidence.

L03 and L06 must additionally show conformance with their targeted amendments
in Sections 3.2 and 3.5. Failure to do so blocks calibration preregistration;
it is not a candidate result.

## 5. Change and claim boundary

This amendment:

- preserves `AE01-H-L01` and every retained P2-I1 identity;
- preserves the original P1-I4 hypothesis file as the historical Phase 1
  freeze;
- adds no schema or reusable implementation;
- does not rename any lane;
- assigns no result, score, recurrence, or N31+ priority; and
- may be superseded only by a recorded pre-execution contract revision for the
  affected future lane.
