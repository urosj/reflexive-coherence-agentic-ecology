## 1. Parent relation and B-R specialization

The frozen parent hypothesis asks whether costly deposition on a non-private
surface can create a dynamic beyond static persistence that changes later
route or continuation eligibility. The post-R3 amendment makes the additional
ecology discriminator explicit:

```text
N30-level persistent trace
!=
repeated costly formation
-> non-static route-local dynamic
-> causal change in later traversal
```

B-R specializes that question without changing it:

```text
repeated native formation into m_e
-> serialized conservative export from m_e to isolated d_e
-> changed current carrier coherence
-> fixed local m_e-to-x_e request
-> native admission, margin movement, or exact field-limited refusal
```

The export policy is producer-mediated. That is an ownership and
naturalization boundary, not an automatic scientific failure. Static
persistence alone remains below L03.

## 2. Operational hypotheses

### `P2-I3-BR-OP-01` — Costly attributable repeated formation

At least two temporally distinguishable native formation transfers debit
registered source `s_e`, credit route-local carrier `m_e`, and retain exact
lineage plus nonzero formation cost. One pulse, free installation, or
unattributed activity cannot satisfy R01.

### `P2-I3-BR-OP-02` — Route-local persistent carrier

After formation stops, `m_e` retains an independently restorable route-local
state through a later checkpoint. The state is neither a private message nor a
route-choice label. This can support R02 but cannot by itself satisfy L03.

### `P2-I3-BR-OP-03` — Conservative non-static export lifecycle

After the repetition floor, at least two serialized lifecycle opportunities
must be addressable and at least one positive eligible export must occur for a
positive non-static-dynamic interpretation. Positive export conserves global
coherence while debiting `m_e` and crediting isolated `d_e`. Eligible zero,
invalid, and duplicate states remain scientifically distinct. Static or
immediate-floor behavior is an informative below-boundary result.

### `P2-I3-BR-OP-04` — Fixed local native encounter

A prospectively fixed `m_e --q_probe--> x_e` request encounters current carrier
coherence without an adapter reading the field, other route, history, labels,
rank, or outcome. Native LGRC9V3 alone admits or refuses. The binary native
decision does not turn the scientific interpretation into a binary gate.

### `P2-I3-BR-OP-05` — Dynamic-to-encounter causal mediation

Lifecycle enable/omission, a registered `C(m_e)` clamp, and a causally inert
`C(d_e)` clamp test whether the B-R dynamic changes the later fixed encounter.
No movement, same-side margin, narrow split, reservoir dependence, and invalid
clamp remain separate interpretations.

### `P2-I3-BR-OP-06` — Formation-quantity temporal discriminator

Repeated formation is compared with one pulse of equal total formation
transfer and cost under
`P2-I3-BR-Q13-FORMATION-QUANTITY-MATCH-001`. Complete current state need not be
equal, so this contrast classifies temporal structure versus accumulated
quantity and cannot alone establish history dependence.

### `P2-I3-BR-OP-07` — Export-mass organization discriminator

Candidate export with `Delta M=-q, Delta O=-q` is compared with conserved
matched removal having `Delta M=-q, Delta O=0` under
`P2-I3-BR-Q13-EXPORT-MASS-MATCH-001`. Control reproduction supports a generic
mass-loss interpretation rather than being discarded as an inconvenient
failure.

### `P2-I3-BR-OP-08` — Complete-state/history discriminator

Different attributable histories with equal causal-continuation projection
and identical future inputs should have equal futures if current composite
state is sufficient. Each arm restores exactly to itself. Verified divergence
reopens active history or missing state; incomplete matching and hidden audit
reads are invalid rather than positive history evidence.

### `P2-I3-BR-OP-09` — Field locality, relocation, and permutation

Moving complete registered current carrier and continuation state between
matched route roles should move the encounter relation. Route labels remain
fixed, role-preserving raw-ID permutation preserves the relation, and isolated
reservoir state does not follow the route into causal participation.

### `P2-I3-BR-OP-10` — Withdrawal, false trace, and producer dependence

Formation withdrawal, false installation through the declared field
interface, static/no-export, and producer omission remain distinct. A false
trace may support current-state sufficiency but cannot inherit costly
formation. Producer dependence is a valid bounded result and naturalization
debt.

### `P2-I3-BR-OP-11` — Hidden-router and participant exclusion

Each route receives an independent, prospectively fixed opportunity from a
matched checkpoint. No selector sees both routes. Participant identity is
audit and eligibility provenance only and cannot select the request or result.
This supports a local relation, not free route choice or planning.

### `P2-I3-BR-OP-12` — Geometry or internal-timescale variation

One preregistered geometry or system-internal timescale variation tests whether
the complete relation persists, changes, narrows, or disappears. No direction
is predetermined. Participant or susceptibility variation alone cannot assign
R05.

### `P2-I3-BR-OP-13` — Trail versus stigmergic interpretation

`trail_field_candidate` requires the bounded route-local field relation.
`stigmergic_field_candidate` additionally requires one participant's
attributable formation to change another eligible, non-addressed participant's
later activity. These are interpretation tags, not new terminal classes or an
R06 rung.

## 6. Open outcomes and redirects

Direction, magnitude, stability, and terminal classification remain open.
Valid outcomes include:

- static trace only;
- positive lifecycle but no encounter movement;
- same-side or narrow encounter movement;
- generic mass-loss reproduction;
- current-state sufficiency;
- verified active-history tension;
- hidden-router or label dependence;
- producer dependence;
- missing intervention, restoration, or native surface;
- geometry/timescale regime limitation; and
- no reusable or generative value.

None may be rewritten after observation merely to obtain a preferred rung.
