## The short version

AE01 asks what operational relations must exist before “agentic ecology” can
mean more than several programmed agents sharing a simulation.

Its first two completed lanes establish two different shared-medium functions:

1. **L01 / P2-I1:** attributable activity changes a non-private medium so that
   the same history creates different later possibilities for matched local
   configurations with different susceptibility.
2. **L02 / P2-I2:** several physical contributions constitute one common
   carrier whose current state, formation history, or both can govern a later
   native response.

Neither result establishes a full ecology, cooperation, coordination, agency,
or an admitted reusable PyGRC primitive. Together they show that shared
substrate history can become participant-relative possibility and that common
carrier organization can compute functions not reducible to arbitrary labels,
private storage, a global variable, or controller-authored success.

P2-I2 also produced an important internal contrast. Appendix A demonstrates
bounded complementarity of three physically distinct operations through a
separate live multi-axis carrier. Appendix B returns the same abstract G/E/P
roles to P2-I2 active history and shows that its proper-subset-positive GEP
result is instead a three-admission terminal-P temporal gate. Similar output
language can therefore hide different carrier computations.

## The common causal architecture

The completed results ask different ecological questions, but they share one
experimental shape:

```text
attributable participant operation(s)
                 |
                 v
        non-private carrier
                 |
                 v
      case-specific carrier proposal
        |-- P2-I1: shared history x local susceptibility
        |-- main P2-I2: state, history, or hybrid
        |-- Appendix A: live multi-axis carrier
        `-- Appendix B: ordered quantitative active history
                 |
                 v
       carrier interventions and
       matched alternative controls
                 |
                 v
        later native response
                 |
                 v
       bounded ecological function
```

The common structure matters because the observed output alone does not name
the mechanism. The carrier geometry, its formation path, the intervention that
breaks it, and the alternatives that reproduce or fail to reproduce it jointly
determine whether the result is differential conditioning, common-carrier
composition, operation complementarity, or temporal gating.

## Why AE01 exists

The conceptual papers describe agents as local differentiations in active
coherence geometry and relation as shared-medium participation. Prior graph
evidence supplies native transport, event histories, traces, eligibility,
feedback, restoration, and other bounded mechanisms. AE01 occupies the gap
between those layers:

```text
conceptual ecological relation
  + admitted graph mechanisms and limits
  + explicit ecology-side construction where needed
  + frozen alternatives and causal controls
  = bounded demand/composition result with visible debt
```

The atlas does not import ecological names into graph evidence. It asks what
additional controlled relation would make each name operational, constructs
that relation honestly, and records whether the substrate already carries it
or an RCAE scaffold remains load-bearing.

## L01 / P2-I1 — shared history becomes differential possibility

### Geometry

P2-I1 uses one attributable writer, a non-private medium outside writer and
reader identities, and matched later reader configurations:

```text
writer activity
  -> ordered native contact/feedback history in shared medium
  -> later reader opportunity
  -> formation or non-formation according to configured susceptibility
```

The writer does not address a future reader or write a success flag. The same
retained medium history is presented to aligned and inverted reader profiles.
The later transition completes through native PyGRC event processing.

### Mechanism shown

P2-I1 goes beyond N30's history-conditioned eligibility by testing an
interaction:

```text
(candidate aligned - withdrawal aligned)
  - (candidate inverted - withdrawal inverted)
```

The interaction is `1` in both contexts at every seed. Susceptibility inversion
reverses which profiles form, so the effect follows the registered relation
rather than a reader slot. Medium withdrawal removes formation; source-order
shuffle preserves row content but removes the registered lineage effect.

### Function unlocked

The bounded function is **shared-medium differential niche conditioning**:
past activity becomes part of the later possibility landscape, and the same
history matters differently to different local configurations.

This is not autonomous niche formation. Writer, medium, feedback surface,
reader profiles, support conditions, and orchestration remain constructed.
Quantity-matched single-writer alternatives also prevent source-plurality,
cooperation, or collective interpretations.

## L02 / P2-I2 — several operations constitute one functional carrier

P2-I2 asks a different question from P2-I1. It is not whether history changes
different readers differently, but whether multiple physical contributions can
become one common state/history on which a later response depends.

For the detailed account of every logical cell, mode, perturbation, control,
appendix, mechanism, function, and future foundation, read the
[P2-I2 interpretative synthesis](reports/P2-I2-interpretative-synthesis.md).

### Common physical opportunity

All three main modes use one matched 23-node, 16-edge opportunity geometry:

```text
S1 --G/+0.625--> P <--P_op/+0.875-- S2
                    |
                    | E/-0.4375
                    v
                   K_P

common arrivals -> optional active ordered history -> M_H

mode-specific front [P], [M_H], or [P,M_H]
  -> native feedback surface and producer
  -> later native 0.125 packet to B, or valid zero
```

Private and diversion routes preserve physical alternatives without giving
the response access to the proposed common carrier. Arbitrary source labels
remain audit lineage, not causal inputs.

### Three retained carrier realizations

| Mode | Carrier | Main result | Implementation status |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| State-carried | Current coherence at `P` | Native common-state composition; both physical orders robustly aligned | Native PyGRC causal path |
| History-carried | Ordered active common history materialized at `M_H` | Order-conditioned relation with causal history dependence | Minimally producer-assisted history adapter; native packets/materialization/feedback/response |
| Hybrid | Current `P` plus materialized `M_H` | Joint state/history factorial relation, also order-conditioned | Same active-history debt plus native state path |

The modes are retained rather than ranked. They expose different questions:
what exists now, how it formed, and how those two surfaces combine.

### Main evidence and controls

The accepted C02 cycle contains 234/234 evaluable terminals, followed by
corrected control resolution and independent retained-evidence reconstruction.
The main L02 result reaches a `supported_bounded_candidate` at a
`scaffold_dependent`, `T3_operational_class` boundary.

Its causal scope is protected by:

- contributor-alone, leave-one-out, order, diversion, and private-partition
  comparisons;
- state-only, history-only, and factorial carrier interventions;
- label, participant rotation, source repetition, alternate access, and
  controller-substitution controls;
- native response-window and packet-gain receipts;
- save/load/reset and continuation identities; and
- complete retained-only reconstruction.

The shared-pool interpretation is about one non-private carrier. It is not
source-diversity synergy: quantity-matched repeated-source cases remain
non-gating and reproduce the relevant output.

## P2-I2 Appendix A — distinct operations in a live multi-axis carrier

Appendix A asks whether generator, extractor, and redistributor operations can
be individually necessary for one carrier function. It uses a different
12-node live carrier with four measured axes and a frozen 19-arm matrix.

All arms are valid. GEP satisfies the bounded carrier envelope; every proper
subset fails; operation-specific and carrier interventions change the result;
participant plurality and the tested adjacent order inversions are
non-load-bearing. Reconstruction is byte-identical and closeout passes 53/53.

This is bounded **operation complementarity**: the three physical operation
types jointly place the live carrier inside one functional region. It remains
a fixture-level result rather than evidence of metabolism, organization, or a
general ecological motif.

## P2-I2 Appendix B — the same roles through P2-I2 carriers

Appendix B projects G/E/P back through the state, active-history, and hybrid
P2-I2 realizations without importing Appendix A outcomes.

APP-B2 initially finds:

| Mode | GEP versus proper subsets |
| --- | --- |
| State | GEP is zero while GP responds; E is suppressive |
| History | GEP responds while all seven proper subsets are zero |
| Hybrid | GEP responds, but P and GP also respond; E is redundant |

APP-B3 verifies the active-history admissions and identifies the missing
matched-cardinality question: does the history result need all three distinct
operation identities, or only some three-token quantitative/order pattern?

APP-B4 answers with 75 fresh PyGRC arms. Over all 24 executable triples and
seeds 101, 211, and 307:

```text
response = 0.125 if and only if the third token is P
```

GGP, GEP, GPP, EGP, EPP, PGP, PEP, and PPP respond. Every feasible G- or
E-terminal triple and all references are zero. EEG, EEE, and EEP are physical
budget exclusions and are not assigned zero.

The mechanism is therefore a **three-admission terminal-P quantitative-history
gate**, not distinct-operation complementarity. The fixed token amounts,
recency fold, materialization, and threshold jointly compute that class; APP-B4
does not yet intervene on them independently.

This contrast is one of AE01's most useful findings:

```text
Appendix A:
  different operations jointly satisfy a live carrier envelope

Appendix B:
  ordered quantitative history selects a terminal-token class
```

Both are real bounded functions. They should not receive the same mechanistic
name merely because GEP is positive in each.

## What L01 and L02 contribute together

The two completed lanes establish a small but meaningful experimental
vocabulary:

| Contribution | Evidence |
| --- | --- |
| Shared substrate history can alter later participant-relative possibility | P2-I1 |
| A common carrier can be constituted by several attributable physical events | Main P2-I2 |
| Current state and formation history can carry different response relations | P2-I2 state/history/hybrid comparison |
| Common and private storage can be causally separated | P2-I2 diversion/private controls |
| Causal provenance can distinguish identical outputs | P2-I1/P2-I2 shuffle, intervention, and controller controls |
| Several distinct operations can jointly satisfy one live carrier function | P2-I2 Appendix A |
| A shared ordered history can implement a terminal-token-qualified gate | P2-I2 Appendix B/APP-B4 |

The common theme is not “agents communicated.” It is:

> A shared medium can preserve enough attributable geometry or history to
> change later local possibility without becoming a direct message or private
> memory.

## Native capability and remaining debt

AE01 follows the native-first rule. Current-state packet composition,
coherence mutation, feedback evaluation, producer scheduling, save/load/reset,
and later packet response are natively expressible in the admitted PyGRC
revision.

The recurring L02 debt is an independently intervenable active shared-history
carrier with ordered admission, exact idempotency/cursor semantics,
clamp/replacement, materialized readout, and restoration. RCAE currently owns
that narrow adapter state in history and hybrid modes. This is a candidate
native requirement, not yet an implementation priority: cross-lane recurrence
has not been tested because L03–L07 remain open.

P2-I1 also retains construction debt around participant/medium roles,
susceptibility profiles, feedback setup, and continuous ecology operation.
Neither lane demonstrates an autonomous long-lived ecology.

## What AE01 has not established

AE01 does not currently establish:

- a complete agentic ecology or admitted ecological motif;
- communication, cooperation, coordination, intention, or collective agency;
- endogenous niche formation or a continuous multi-cycle shared-pool ecology;
- cross-lane recurrence of one native requirement;
- trails with decay/reinforcement/maintenance dynamics;
- support-field economy, maintained selective boundaries, or budget-closing
  circulation;
- parent-basin modulation above the inherited ceiling; or
- an executed, admitted, or RCAE-consumed N31 coherence-derived relation,
  closure, or decay primitive.

The atlas remains at `AE01-C2` precisely because five lanes and the later
synthesis, control/debt, and selection gates remain open.

## What happens next

P2-I3 first waits for graph-project N31 to test whether ordinary `C/J_C`
evolution supplies causal decay without additional state, then discriminate
and, if warranted, specify a closure, extension, or decay primitive. It then
resumes through the exact source-transition and Q-005-through-Q-008 procedure
in the
[handoff contract](implementation/P2-I3-N31-decay-primitive-handoff.md); N31
cannot discharge the lane's ecology topology, traversal encounter, controls,
or result. L04–L07 must likewise define and test their ecology-specific
discriminators rather than replay inherited graph capabilities. Only after all
terminal records exist may AE01 compare normalized requirements, use leave-
one-lane-out recurrence controls, assess shared fixture dependence, and either
select another graph-side target or close with explicit non-selection.

Possible outcomes remain:

- one recurring substrate demand worth a new graph experiment;
- several useful but non-recurring lane-local constructions;
- precise missing-surface specifications without implementation priority; or
- honest non-selection.
