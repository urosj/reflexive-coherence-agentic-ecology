## 1. Why this record exists

The word `decay` does not identify one physical, geometric, or computational
operation. During Q-005 discussion, three materially different
interpretations emerged:

```text
release-efficacy attenuation
coherence-conserving source leakage
route-susceptibility relaxation
```

All three can make a later route effect become weaker. They do not therefore
describe the same field, continuation state, topology, intervention, or claim.
Treating them as interchangeable implementations of one scalar decay factor
would hide the most important scientific question: **what changed, where did
it go, and why did later traversal change?**

This report preserves:

- the exact meaning of each proposal;
- what existing RC theory, LGRC9V3, PyGRC, and predecessor experiments do and
  do not support;
- conservation, time, topology, restoration, and local-encounter boundaries;
- the evidence each realization could produce;
- failure and confound interpretations;
- why the proposed implementations are separate causal realizations; and
- which infrastructure may safely be shared without assuming topology or
  semantic equivalence.

The report is deliberately prior to Q-006. Equations below are relation
sketches used to expose required state and invariants. They are not frozen
numeric laws.

## 2. Non-negotiable common boundaries

### 2.1 LGRC9V3 is the core substrate

Under `P2-I3-DEC-020`, only LGRC9V3 can satisfy the core substrate role.
Synchronous GRC9V3 evolution may provide a comparative result but cannot
replace or bridge LGRC9V3 because its step and relaxation semantics differ.

Adequate public LGRC9V3 transitions retain priority. A missing lifecycle or
encounter operation does not weaken the experiment; it becomes an explicit
RCAE-owned producer or constructed mechanism plus graph-side naturalization
debt under `P2-I3-DEC-017` and `P2-I3-DEC-022`.

### 2.2 Coherence is not destroyed

The admitted distance theory states the continuity relation

```text
partial_t C + divergence(J_C) = 0.
```

For the source-current LGRC9V3 packet substrate, the corresponding audited
budget is:

```text
sum(node coherence) + sum(in-flight packet coherence) = constant
```

within the registered closed system and numeric tolerance.

Consequently:

- an in-flight packet never loses amplitude merely because time elapsed;
- a departure debits exactly the amount placed in flight;
- an arrival credits exactly that amount;
- any local loss of node coherence requires a declared destination; and
- any non-coherence field state requires its own invariant and cost semantics
  rather than being smuggled into the coherence budget.

### 2.3 Time is generated by the system

Decay may depend on LGRC9V3 event order, event-time keys, node proper time, or
another registered system-internal clock. It may not depend on wall-clock
duration, process scheduling, test-run duration, or unrecorded external time.

An internal time value is necessary but not sufficient. Q-006 must identify:

- which clock owns the transition;
- which event advances it;
- whether idle progression is meaningful;
- what is restored after save/load and branch; and
- how equal-input continuation is proved.

### 2.4 The route effect must be local

The later traversal must encounter the field through the selected route-local
surface. None of the three interpretations may be implemented as:

```text
read both route scores
choose the larger score
write the preferred result
```

Route names, formation addresses, depositor labels, success labels, and
precomputed preference values are forbidden inputs to the later response.

### 2.5 A weakening response is not automatically field decay

The experiment must distinguish:

```text
field-state decay
field-expression decay
material redistribution
relational relaxation
probe depletion
participant adaptation
generic elapsed-time response change
```

A downstream response becoming smaller is evidence only for response change
until the registered mediator, intervention, and causal chain identify what
carried that change.

## 3. Source-current evidence boundary

### 3.1 RC-Distance

The admitted
`geometric-reflexive-coherence:investigations/2026-01-RC-Distance-v4.md`
supports these statements:

- geometry is derived from coherence, its gradient, and current;
- the induced metric is indexed by current state;
- a flux term introduces directional anisotropy;
- sustained coherent transport can create highway-like directional
  structure; and
- geometric distance and operational travel time are distinct.

It does **not** define:

- a slow route-local susceptibility variable;
- a route-use reinforcement law for such a variable;
- persistence of that variable after flux stops;
- autonomous relaxation of that variable toward baseline; or
- a native LGRC9V3 conductance-memory policy.

The paper also limits a tempting inference: anisotropic metric structure does
not by itself prove lower operational distance. Transport speed and the
chosen timing relation remain load-bearing.

### 3.2 Public LGRC9V3/PyGRC packet and timing surfaces

The accepted I01/I02 source records establish public support for:

- positive packet amounts fixed before departure;
- exact source debit, in-flight accounting, and target credit;
- deterministic queue and event ordering;
- event-time and node-proper-time state;
- public snapshot/load/reset and continuation surfaces; and
- LGRC-visible node coherence and edge/base/port conductance state.

They do not establish a native policy for:

- time-conditioned packet release efficacy;
- autonomous leakage scheduling;
- route-use-conditioned conductance reinforcement;
- conductance or susceptibility relaxation; or
- a local traversal response that consumes any of those field states without
  an RCAE completion.

Thus all three proposals require some RCAE-owned policy. They differ greatly
in how much of the state transition remains native after that policy acts.

### 3.3 N08 and N22

The admitted N08 Hypothesis B record permits declared geometry, topology,
support, or conductance to carry trail memory. It requires an explicit
conserved destination when decay moves mass. Its closeout nevertheless records:

```text
native_route_conductance_memory_policy_missing
```

and does not establish native conductance update, strengthening, relaxation,
or a persistent pure-flux trail.

N22 reaches a bounded producer-mediated susceptibility/durable-geometry
candidate through LGRC-visible edge/base/port conductance deltas. It explicitly
retains:

```text
native_route_conductance_memory_supported = false
native route-conductance update = naturalization debt
```

N22 is therefore a construction and control precedent for a susceptibility
realization. It is not evidence that slow susceptibility relaxation is already
an LGRC theory or runtime primitive, and it supplies no P2-I3 result.

## 4. Proposal A — release-efficacy attenuation

### 4.1 Core interpretation

```text
carrier coherence remains locally available
+ elapsed internal field age or phase changes release policy
-> newly created response packets carry less coherence
-> later field expression becomes weaker
```

This proposal originated from the valid observation that any amplitude
reduction must occur when a new packet is formed, not while an existing packet
is in flight. A conceptual relation is:

```text
q_release = release_policy(
  current carrier coherence,
  registered internal age or phase,
  field bounds,
  encounter inputs
)
```

Once `q_release` is selected, native packet mechanics debit, transport, and
credit that exact amount. The policy may choose a smaller future amount; the
runtime may not transform an already-created amount into a smaller arrival.

### 4.2 What actually decays

The carrier amount need not decay. The decaying object is **release efficacy**:
the relation between current carrier state and its later expression.

That creates a continuation-state obligation. If two routes have the same
node coherence but different later release efficacy, some state must explain
the difference, for example:

- last valid formation proper time;
- an internal field-age variable;
- a release phase or policy cursor; or
- active scheduled history.

That state is causally load-bearing and must be registered, restored,
intervened on, relocated where applicable, and included in complete-state
matching. Calling it merely “elapsed time” does not make it non-state.

### 4.3 Native and RCAE ownership

| Function | Ownership/status |
| --- | --- |
| Node coherence carrier | Native LGRC9V3 |
| Event/proper-time inputs | Native LGRC9V3 |
| Packet amount, debit, in-flight state, arrival | Native LGRC9V3 after amount selection |
| Age/phase-to-release law | RCAE producer-assisted or constructed |
| Causal age/phase state | RCAE unless a later native surface is found |
| Local traversal encounter | RCAE completion required |
| Restoration of composite state | Native runtime plus RCAE wrapper for non-native state |

### 4.4 Conservation and accounting

This proposal can preserve coherence exactly:

```text
source debit = created packet amount = target credit
```

A smaller packet means less coherence was transferred; it does not mean the
difference disappeared. Remaining coherence stays at the source unless a
separate declared transition moves it.

The implementation must not report

```text
unreleased coherence = decayed coherence
```

because no coherence decay occurred.

### 4.5 Topology implications

The selected node-carried two-route kernel may be usable, but this is not
automatic. The topology must make a locally formed response packet affect
later traversal without a global route selector. If release is directed to a
probe or response node, that receiving relation must be part of the registered
local encounter rather than a disguised route choice.

Topology reuse with another proposal requires demonstrated equivalence. The
presence of two alternative routes alone is insufficient.

### 4.6 Required controls

- Match carrier coherence while varying only registered release age/phase.
- Match age/phase while varying carrier coherence.
- Freeze the release policy at candidate and reference efficacy.
- Restore and branch identical composite state, then verify equal
  continuation.
- Install current efficacy without formation history to test current-state
  sufficiency.
- Remove age/phase input while preserving carrier and traversal opportunity.
- Verify exact packet conservation and the absence of in-flight attenuation.
- Exclude route labels and global comparison from amount selection.

### 4.7 What a result would mean

A clean result would establish a bounded **decay-of-field-expression**
candidate. It would not by itself show that local field magnitude decreased or
that route geometry relaxed.

This distinction is useful as a diagnostic: it separates a persistent field
whose ability to act changes from a field whose carrier state itself changes.
It is weaker as the primary trail ontology because the load-bearing age/phase
state can become an independent hidden memory surface.

### 4.8 Principal risks

- The policy may amount to an external timer multiplying an output.
- The age variable may become an undeclared second carrier.
- Lower packet amount may be mistaken for coherence loss.
- A directly addressed response packet may bypass local traversal.
- A fitted attenuation curve may manufacture the expected dynamic.

### 4.9 Current disposition

Retain as a separately rerunnable interpretation and a valuable discriminator
between carrier decay and expression decay. It is not currently proposed as
one of the two primary implementations.

## 5. Proposal B — coherence-conserving source leakage

### 5.1 Core interpretation

```text
route-local carrier coherence drives an endogenous emission
-> a native packet is created
-> the carrier node is debited exactly
-> coherence enters declared in-flight state
-> a declared receiver is credited exactly
-> local trail magnitude falls while total coherence remains invariant
```

“Leakage” here means local departure from the route carrier, not disappearance
from the closed system. A conceptual relation is:

```text
q_leak = leak_policy(
  max(W_route - W_floor, 0),
  internal elapsed time,
  registered bounds
)
```

with:

```text
0 <= q_leak <= max(W_route - W_floor, 0)
```

The exact law, schedule, floor, and amount belong to Q-006. The important
semantic commitment is that the emitter's internal coherence state determines
the possible release, while native packet transfer carries the quantity.

### 5.2 What actually decays

The route-local node-coherence carrier decreases. The system-wide coherence
budget does not.

This is genuine local field-state decay through redistribution:

```text
W_route decreases
W_in_flight or W_receiver increases by the same amount
```

The receiver and any later return path are scientifically relevant. They
cannot be omitted simply because the primary metric reads the route node.

### 5.3 Native and RCAE ownership

| Function | Ownership/status |
| --- | --- |
| Route-local node carrier | Native LGRC9V3 under DEC-021 |
| Packet amount representation | Native LGRC9V3 |
| Departure debit and in-flight transition | Native LGRC9V3 |
| Arrival credit | Native LGRC9V3 |
| Queue/event/proper-time state | Native LGRC9V3 |
| Leak trigger and amount policy | RCAE producer-assisted |
| Leak destination role and exclusion rules | RCAE experiment definition |
| Local carrier-to-traversal response | RCAE completion required |
| Native state restoration | Native LGRC9V3 |
| Restoration of producer cursor/policy state | RCAE wrapper if such state exists |

Compared with release attenuation, this path can avoid an independent
efficacy carrier: current node coherence plus registered native time and any
minimal producer cursor may be sufficient. Q-006 must prove rather than assume
that sufficiency.

### 5.4 Conservation and accounting

Every emitted packet requires an auditable chain:

```text
pre-departure route coherence
- exact packet amount
= post-departure route coherence

pre-arrival receiver coherence
+ exact same packet amount
= post-arrival receiver coherence
```

The complete audit includes queued and in-flight packets. A leak packet must
not be silently deleted, attenuated in flight, or excluded from the system
budget.

### 5.5 Topology implications

This realization likely needs topology beyond a bare two-route diamond:

- one exclusive carrier node per alternative route;
- one or more declared leak receivers, reservoirs, or return surfaces;
- route-symmetric receiver opportunity unless asymmetry is an explicit
  registered factor;
- isolation preventing receiver state from independently selecting a route;
- no unintended return flow that maintains the carrier; and
- a local encounter path through which the probe experiences current carrier
  state.

Whether a common neutral reservoir, paired route-local reservoirs, or another
closed receiver structure is correct remains open. That decision affects
symmetry, interference, restoration, and what “leakage” means. It belongs to
the leakage realization's topology freeze, not to this report.

### 5.6 Required controls

- Disable only emission while preserving route activity, time, and
  opportunity.
- Divert the same emitted quantity to a matched alternative receiver.
- Clamp route carrier coherence while leaving emission scheduling intact.
- Match route carrier state with different prior leakage histories.
- Withdraw depositor activity and observe autonomous internal-time emission.
- Restore before a scheduled emission and verify equal event continuation.
- Relocate complete current carrier and producer continuation state where the
  registered mode makes relocation valid.
- Verify total node-plus-in-flight coherence at every transition.
- Verify the receiver cannot act as a hidden route selector or feedback
  source.

### 5.7 What a result would mean

A clean result would show a bounded trail field whose local strength decays by
conserved redistribution after formation stops, and whose current local state
causally changes later traversal.

It would not show:

- coherence destruction;
- geometric or susceptibility relaxation;
- a native autonomous leakage primitive;
- a general resource economy; or
- a stigmergic interpretation without the separately required participant
  relation.

### 5.8 Principal risks

- The leak receiver may become a hidden second field or route cue.
- Scheduled emissions may be an external clock in disguise.
- Emission may consume the carrier so rapidly that the result is merely
  depletion.
- A producer may precompute the desired trajectory rather than respond to
  source state.
- Probe packets and lifecycle leak packets may be conflated.
- A floor or return flow may silently maintain the field.

### 5.9 Current disposition

This is the strongest native-mechanics-aligned candidate exposed by the Q-005
discussion. It preserves the DEC-021 node carrier and uses native LGRC9V3
conservation and packet transitions for the load-bearing movement, but its
autonomous emission policy remains `producer_assisted`, not native decay.

Under DEC-023 it is now an N31 primitive candidate, not an authorized P2-I3
implementation.

## 6. Proposal C — relaxing route susceptibility

### 6.1 Two distinct forms

The phrase “geometric relaxation” hides two different mechanisms.

#### C1 — purely derived instantaneous geometry

```text
g(t) = geometry(C(t), gradient(C(t)), j(t))
```

When sustaining current changes or stops, the flux-derived anisotropic term
changes with it. This is directly aligned with the admitted RC-Distance
construction.

Its limitation is decisive for L03: absent another slow state, the highway
effect may vanish immediately with current. It can show current-indexed
directional geometry but may provide no durable-yet-decaying aftereffect after
formation activity stops.

C1 is therefore best treated as a theory-grounded diagnostic or future
comparative realization unless a source-current implementation demonstrates a
continuation-relevant aftereffect without inventing one.

#### C2 — slow susceptibility/conductance aftereffect

```text
repeated route use updates S_e
S_e affects route conductance or another local coupling
without reinforcement S_e relaxes toward S_base
later traversal locally encounters the changed coupling
```

A conceptual relation is:

```text
S_e(next) = bounded_update(
  S_e(current),
  attributable completed route-use events,
  internal elapsed time,
  reinforcement,
  relaxation,
  saturation
)
```

and separately:

```text
route coupling = Psi(S_e, current LGRC state)
```

Neither equation nor `Psi` is defined by current LGRC theory, specification,
or runtime. They would be RCAE constructions.

### 6.2 What actually decays

In C2, coherence need not move. The decaying object is a bounded relational
state that modifies route coupling.

That state has its own invariants:

- registered baseline and bounds;
- update only from declared attributable events;
- relaxation only under the declared internal-time law;
- no hidden route or outcome labels;
- exact snapshot/load/branch identity;
- explicit cost, if modification is costly; and
- exact mapping to an LGRC-visible conductance or other local substrate
  surface.

`S_e` must not be an opaque `memory_strength` consulted by a global router.

### 6.3 Carrier and ownership consequence

Slow susceptibility is not merely a lifecycle attached to DEC-021's node
carrier. If `S_e` persists after node coherence is matched and it determines
later traversal, `S_e` is a continuation-relevant causal carrier.

Therefore a C2 implementation requires its own realization/campaign identity
and a controlled reopening of Q-003/Q-004 for that campaign. Its likely
ownership is:

| Function | Ownership/status |
| --- | --- |
| Coherence, packets, attributable route-use receipts, internal time | Native LGRC9V3 |
| Edge/base/port conductance representation | LGRC-visible native state |
| Meaning of conductance delta as susceptibility | RCAE operational interpretation |
| Route-use-conditioned update | RCAE constructed producer |
| Autonomous relaxation toward baseline | RCAE constructed producer |
| Bounds, saturation, cost, and update phase | RCAE constructed |
| Local conductance-to-traversal response | RCAE completion unless native conformance proves otherwise |
| Composite restoration/intervention | Native state plus RCAE wrapper |

This preserves N22's strongest precedent while retaining its naturalization
debt.

### 6.4 Conservation and accounting

Susceptibility is not coherence mass. Its relaxation neither creates nor
destroys coherence, provided the actual LGRC coherence budget remains closed.

If route-use reinforcement has a cost, the implementation must account for
that cost through a separately declared native transfer or other accepted
mechanism. A free producer update cannot satisfy costly formation merely
because the resulting conductance is stored in LGRC-visible state.

### 6.5 Topology implications

The susceptibility realization must not inherit the leakage topology by
default. It may require:

- two baseline-matchable edge or corridor sequences;
- edge/base/port conductance surfaces that can vary without changing route
  identity;
- enough route extent to distinguish local coupling from an endpoint effect;
- a traversal mechanism that encounters each changed coupling locally;
- symmetric formation opportunity and attributable route-use receipts;
- fixed registered topology during relaxation and relocation controls; and
- a complete edge/corridor state payload for snapshot and relocation.

A simple two-route graph may eventually satisfy both realizations, but that is
a conformance result to demonstrate, not a design premise. Carrier location,
receiver requirements, continuation state, and encounter mechanics differ.

### 6.6 Required controls

- Susceptibility clamp at candidate and baseline while matching coherence.
- Coherence clamp while varying susceptibility.
- Static positive conductance installed without route-use history.
- Quantity-matched route use under different temporal formation.
- Relaxation disabled while all other state and elapsed internal time match.
- Route-use receipts present versus removed from the update input.
- Complete susceptibility/conductance relocation without route labels.
- Snapshot/load/branch identity including update phase and producer state.
- No global route comparison or score consultation.
- Measurement separation among registered conductance, geometric distance,
  functional distance, causal/proper-time distance, and experimental causal
  influence.

### 6.7 What a result would mean

A clean result would support a bounded **RCAE-constructed route-susceptibility
relaxation candidate** coupled to LGRC-visible state. It could expose a precise
missing graph-side lifecycle pattern.

It would not establish:

- native LGRC susceptibility memory;
- a theory-derived slow state;
- native geometric relaxation;
- shorter operational distance merely from a metric delta;
- semantic learning; or
- a general ecology primitive independent of realization debt.

### 6.8 Principal risks

- `S_e` may be an arbitrary memory score with geometric vocabulary.
- The producer may carry the result rather than LGRC-visible state.
- Conductance may not causally affect the actual local traversal path.
- Current-state matching may omit update phase or hidden producer history.
- “Geometry” may be claimed from conductance without computing the admitted
  geometric surface.
- A fitted relaxation law may be tuned to mirror the leakage outcome.

### 6.9 Current disposition

Retain C2 as a graph-side primitive candidate, explicitly classified at entry
as an RCAE-constructed mechanism with N08/N22 precedent and naturalization
debt. N31 must decide whether it can be theory-authorized and naturalized,
remains producer-mediated, or should be rejected. It requires its own topology
and cannot inherit the leakage fixture.

## 8. Why the N31 candidates must remain separate

The leakage and susceptibility realizations test different ontologies:

```text
leakage:
  local field quantity moves through the conserved coherence economy

susceptibility:
  a non-coherence relational state changes and relaxes
```

They cannot be represented as two values of one `decay_mode` parameter inside
one scientific matrix without obscuring:

- which state is the carrier;
- what topology makes the transition closed;
- what is conserved;
- what counts as complete current state;
- what must be restored and relocated;
- how later traversal encounters the field;
- which controls are valid; and
- which claim ceiling applies.

They therefore require separate realization identities inside N31 even if
some infrastructure is shared. N31 may select one, more than one, or none; the
comparison must not presuppose that both become P2-I3 implementations.

## 9. Safe reuse without assuming common topology

### 9.1 Share the abstract protocol

The N31 realizations can implement the same high-level causal roles:

```text
construct registered baseline
form route-local field through attributable costly repeated activity
stop depositor activity
advance registered internal dynamics
perform declared intervention
introduce fresh later traversal opportunity
observe field and traversal surfaces
snapshot, restore, branch, and reconstruct
```

The role names and ordering may be shared. Their concrete operations may not.

### 9.2 Share artifact envelopes and validation infrastructure

Safe candidates for reuse include:

- source, environment, runtime, policy, and commit identity envelopes;
- canonical serialization and digest helpers;
- attempt and terminal-record machinery;
- common event-receipt envelopes with realization-specific payloads;
- common observation-panel names;
- schema dispatch by explicit `realization_id` and `schema_version`;
- reconstruction and byte-identity tooling;
- threshold-ladder and developmental-interpretation utilities;
- report generation from machine facts; and
- common hidden-router and prohibited-input guards.

The common layer must not assume that a route is an edge, a node, or a fixed
path sequence.

### 9.3 Use realization adapters, not one universal state

A future source layout may expose a small protocol such as:

```text
build_registered_topology()
form_field()
stop_formation()
advance_internal_dynamics()
observe_field_state()
perform_local_traversal()
apply_intervention()
snapshot_complete_state()
restore_complete_state()
audit_invariants()
```

The leakage adapter and susceptibility adapter provide different topology,
state, transitions, and invariant audits. No common implementation should
read route scores and decide outcomes on their behalf.

This interface is illustrative only. Exact API and module names require a
later implementation decision.

### 9.4 Keep these parts realization-specific

- topology fixture and symmetry proof;
- carrier schema and units;
- Q-006 equation and update order;
- internal-time binding;
- local encounter implementation;
- complete continuation-state inventory;
- intervention and relocation payload;
- conformance assertions;
- calibration null generator;
- numeric registration and thresholds;
- candidate matrix and seeds;
- control applicability and outcomes; and
- terminal claim language.

Duplicating these pieces is not wasted effort. They are the scientific content
that proves the two mechanisms are genuinely different.

- Treat release attenuation, conserved leakage, and susceptibility relaxation
  as distinct semantic candidates.
- Give each candidate its smallest faithful topology and invariant contract.
- Use source-current evidence, local causal readout, intervention, restoration,
  and reconstruction.
- Select one primitive, multiple distinct primitives, a producer-only
  candidate, or no primitive without forcing continuity.

  and public operation in graph-project scope.

### P2-I3 — later ecology composition

- Retain Q-005 as unresolved during N31.
- Admit the exact N31 closeout through a new source-transition package.
- Reopen DEC-021/022 only where the returned primitive changes carrier or
  ownership facts.
- Select the L03 dynamic only after N31 evidence is reconstructed.
- Design the two-route ecology topology, local encounter, controls,
  calibration, and execution inside P2-I3; N31 does not discharge them.

This structure avoids making RCAE the author of unresolved substrate semantics
while preserving its freedom to construct an ecology-side mechanism if N31
selects nothing.

## 12. Interpretation ladder

Thresholds remain guidance bands under the developmental-interpretation
contract, not automatic accept/reject switches.

For either implementation, interpretation must ask:

- Did the selected state actually change?
- Was the change distinguishable from measurement resolution?
- Was it directionally strong, narrow, mixed, or unexpected?
- Did the local traversal relation change with it?
- Did mediator intervention move or remove the traversal effect?
- Did matched controls expose an alternative explanation?
- Was the effect robust to the registered perturbation or variation?
- Would a local refinement address a narrow result without changing the
  mechanism, or did the evidence expose a different mechanism entirely?

A small or counter-directional effect is not discarded. It receives a
structural and behavioral interpretation and a bounded next-move record.

## 14. Bottom line

The three proposals answer different questions:

```text
release attenuation:
  Does an old field express less even when its carrier remains?

conserved leakage:
  Does local field magnitude fall because coherence leaves through a closed
  native transfer path?

susceptibility relaxation:
  Does a constructed route relation return toward baseline while coherence
  may remain unchanged?
```

The active program sends all three meanings to N31 for a small, directed
LGRC9V3 primitive experiment. Conserved leakage enters as the strongest
native-mechanics-aligned candidate; susceptibility relaxation enters as an
explicitly constructed naturalization candidate; release attenuation enters
as a boundary between field decay and expression decay. P2-I3 then consumes
the bounded graph result rather than defining the primitive locally.
