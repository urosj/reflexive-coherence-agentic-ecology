- **Mechanism:** what the registered interventions and exclusions warrant as

## 2. The central P2-I2 finding

The simplest statement of the main result is:

> Two attributable physical contributions can enter one non-private carrier,
> lose their arbitrary source labels at the causal read boundary, and jointly
> change a later native response. What counts as the carrier can be current
> state, active ordered history, or both.

This is more than two successful writes. The response does not independently
query S1 and S2, read private mailboxes, count contributors, or accept a
controller-authored success flag. It encounters one declared common carrier.
Removing either contribution, preventing common admission, clamping the
mode-relevant carrier, or replacing it with a legitimate private partition
breaks the candidate relation. Changing attribution-only labels does not.

The result is nevertheless narrower than cooperation or collective memory.
Quantity-matched repetitions by S1 alone or S2 alone reproduce the response.
The load-bearing fact is therefore the constitution of one carrier by the two
registered contribution operations—not the plurality, identity, intention, or
functional diversity of their original sources.

The appendices sharpen a second distinction. Appendix A shows a separate live
carrier in which three physically different operations are jointly necessary
for one bounded functional envelope. Appendix B initially reproduces a
proper-subset-positive GEP relation through P2-I2 active history, but APP-B4
shows that this relation belongs to a broader three-admission terminal-P class.
Carrier composition and operation complementarity are therefore separable:
one can be real while the other is absent.

## 3. Main P2-I2 geometry

### 3.1 Physical opportunity topology

All three main modes share one matched 23-node, 16-edge physical opportunity
topology. The causal carrier and response mask change by mode, but the physical
contribution opportunities do not.

```text
                         q1 = 0.625
                S1 --------------------> P
                 |                        ^
                 | diversion             |
                 v                        | q2 = 0.875
              K_DIV_Q1                   S2
                                          |
                                          | diversion
                                          v
                                       K_DIV_Q2

private alternative:
                S1 -> P1_PRIVATE      S2 -> P2_PRIVATE

state intervention:
                P  -> K_P             native debit = 0.4375

history materialization:
                admitted common arrivals -> active H_P
                active H_P -> ordered fold -> native packet -> M_H

later response:
                mode-specific common front
                  [P] or [M_H] or [P, M_H]
                              |
                              v
                native feedback surface against B_REF
                              |
                              v
                PyGRC producer schedules 0.125 packet
                from A_PRIMARY or A_ALTERNATE to B
```

`P` begins at coherence `0.75`. S1 and S2 begin at `7.5`. Their common
contributions are native packet transfers into `P`; diversion and private
routes are also native packet paths. The measured outcome is the later native
coherence gain at `B`: either `0.0` or `0.125` in the retained matrix.

`B` is geometrically separate from `P`, `M_H`, private carriers, and the
reference node. It receives only the registered later response packet. This
separates carrier constitution from response measurement.

### 3.2 One carrier and two kinds of identity

P2-I2 deliberately separates causal identity from audit identity:

```text
causal carrier identity:
  the current P state, active source-label-free H_P/M_H, or both

audit lineage:
  which physical source, route, amount, event, and order produced each change
```

The audit can still attribute q1 to S1 and q2 to S2. The later response path
does not read those labels. This is the key factorization that lets a carrier
be common without making provenance disappear.

### 3.3 What “non-private” means here

Non-private does not mean globally accessible. It means that the registered
response encounters the common carrier through a carrier-scoped mask rather
than a contributor address. Both the primary and one alternate eligible
responder reproduce the relation through the same common mask. In contrast,
each legitimate private response mask is confined to `P1_PRIVATE` or
`P2_PRIVATE`, or their corresponding private histories, and returns zero.

The demonstrated access transfer is therefore one bounded alternate-responder
contrast, not universal availability.

## 4. The three main causal realizations

The modes are alternative causal organizations of the same physical writes.
They are not a ranking and should not be averaged into one mechanism.

### 4.1 State-carried: the pool is its present condition

**Observed geometry.** Both native packets accumulate in the same scalar
carrier `P`. The later response front reads `P` only. Packet/event history is
retained for audit but excluded from the causal response mask.

```text
q1 + q2 -> current P -> native thresholded feedback -> B +0.125
```

Both q1-then-q2 and q2-then-q1 return `0.125` at every seed. Each single
contribution and each symmetric leave-one admission returns `0.0`. A native
post-write debit from `P` to `K_P` changes the response from `0.125` to `0.0`.
Changing physical order or attribution-only history while preserving `P` does
not change the response.

**Mechanism shown.** This is an order-insensitive accumulation-to-eligibility
mechanism. The complete present carrier condition is causal. Formation order
is not.

**Bounded functionality unlocked.** Several physical contributions can create
one current shared condition that gates a later action without retaining
source identity in the read path. This is a native PyGRC causal expression in
the frozen fixture.

**Possible foundation.** This could ground shared accumulators, quorum-like
eligibility surfaces, bounded reserve thresholds, or common-state control
signals. Those applications would require their own semantics, economy, and
scale tests.

### 4.2 History-carried: the pool is an active ordered formation record

**Observed geometry.** Native arrivals on the two registered common routes are
admitted into one active, source-label-free history `H_P`. An ordered fold with
recency coefficient `0.375` is materialized by a native packet into `M_H`.
The later response reads `M_H` only; current `P` is excluded.

```text
native q events -> active ordered H_P -> materialized M_H
                                        |
                                        v
                              native feedback -> B
```

q1-then-q2 returns `0.125` at every seed; q2-then-q1 returns `0.0`. Clamping
the history to its reference condition returns `0.0`. Debiting current `P`
while holding `H_P/M_H` fixed leaves the response at `0.125`. Source-label
permutation is invariant.

**Mechanism shown.** The causal object is not a record that an external
controller later queries. It is active, independently intervenable runtime
state whose temporal ordering is folded into a native readout. The same final
current-state opportunity can therefore lead to a different response because
its formation history differs.

**Bounded functionality unlocked.** The carrier can encode a source-label-
independent temporal pattern and make later eligibility depend on formation
order rather than only current quantity.

**Possible foundation.** This could ground temporal eligibility, sequence-
sensitive shared context, recency-weighted environmental traces, or history-
conditioned control. The load-bearing `H_P/M_H` leg is still owned by
`RCAEActiveHistoryAdapterV2`, so the immediate graph-side foundation is a
native active-history carrier, not a claim that these higher functions already
exist natively.

### 4.3 Hybrid: the pool is present state and formation history together

**Observed geometry.** The physical writes change native `P` and are also
admitted to active `H_P`, which materializes into `M_H`. The later response
reads `[P, M_H]` exactly once each through one native joint feedback surface.

```text
q1 + q2 -> current P -----------+
                                    +-> joint native feedback -> B
q1 + q2 -> ordered H_P -> M_H --+
```

The complete q1-then-q2 carrier returns `0.125`; the reverse order returns
`0.0`. The frozen 2 × 2 component intervention is exact:

| P component | H_P/M_H component | Response |
| --- | --- | ---: |
| candidate | candidate | `0.125` |
| reference | candidate | `0.0` |
| candidate | reference | `0.0` |
| reference | reference | `0.0` |

**Mechanism shown.** Current state and active history are separately necessary
components of the same response path. Present geometry supplies magnitude;
formation geometry supplies a temporally selective condition. Neither alone
is sufficient in the registered factorial.

**Bounded functionality unlocked.** The carrier can implement conjunctive
state-and-history gating: respond only when both the current shared condition
and its accepted formation history are present.

**Possible foundation.** This could support bounded context-sensitive control,
state-qualified temporal patterns, or mechanisms that distinguish “same
amount, different way of arriving.” A native implementation again requires a
graph-owned active-history component; the joint PyGRC response itself is
already adequate.

## 5. What every main experimental case contributes

All main responses below are identical across seeds 101, 211, and 307.

### 5.1 Reference pool: establish that opportunity alone is inert

Cases:

- `reference_pool_empty`: no contribution, matched neutral opportunity;
- `reference_pool_write_frozen`: both sources remain active and pay their
  debits, but both writes go to inert diversion sinks.

All three modes return `0.0` in both cases.

**Geometric meaning.** The responder opportunity, clock, source activity, and
packet machinery do not create a response without common-carrier
constitution.

**Mechanism excluded.** Scheduling, neutral contact, or source activity alone
cannot explain the candidate.

**Function established.** Scientific zero is a valid carrier condition, not a
failed execution.

### 5.2 Individual contributions: distinguish contribution count from source plurality

Cases and responses in every mode:

| Case | Response |
| --- | ---: |
| S1 supplies q1 only | `0.0` |
| S2 supplies q2 only | `0.0` |
| S1 supplies both quantity-matched physical slots | `0.125` |
| S2 supplies both quantity-matched physical slots | `0.125` |

**Geometric meaning.** One original contribution does not reach the registered
carrier condition. One capacity-relaxed source executing both registered
physical contribution slots does.

**Mechanism shown.** The response depends on contribution structure in the
common carrier, not on a count of distinct source identities.

**Function established.** The carrier accepts multiple attributable inputs
without requiring multiple actors as a load-bearing condition.

**Claim limit.** This blocks functional source diversity, cooperation,
coordination, or coalition interpretations in the main experiment.

### 5.3 Combined orders: reveal carrier temporality

| Mode | q1 then q2 | q2 then q1 | Interpretation |
| --- | ---: | ---: | --- |
| State-carried | `0.125` | `0.125` | Equal final P gives equal response |
| History-carried | `0.125` | `0.0` | Ordered H_P is causal |
| Hybrid | `0.125` | `0.0` | History order conditions the joint P + M_H read |

**Geometric meaning.** The same physical quantities and slots can define
different causal objects depending on whether the carrier forgets or retains
formation order.

**Mechanism shown.** Order sensitivity is a property of carrier identity, not
an execution inconsistency.

**Function established.** P2-I2 can express both commutative pooling and
noncommutative temporal pooling over matched physical operations.

### 5.4 Pooled-history shuffle: separate physical order from arbitrary labels

The physical-order-reversal rows reproduce the same mode-specific pattern as
the combined-order rows. Audit-lineage label permutation returns the candidate
response in all modes.

**Geometric meaning.** Reordering physical events can alter active history;
renaming sources while leaving event physics unchanged cannot.

**Mechanism shown.** History and hybrid order effects are carried by event
amount/timing/order, not source-name lookup.

**Function established.** The common carrier can preserve causally relevant
temporal structure while remaining invariant to attribution-only metadata.

### 5.5 Contributor removal: show joint constitution relative to the two registered writes

For both physical orders and all three modes:

```text
q1 admitted, q2 diverted -> 0.0
q2 admitted, q1 diverted -> 0.0
both admitted             -> mode-specific candidate response
label-only permutation    -> candidate response
```

**Geometric meaning.** Each registered contribution changes the common carrier
while the removed contribution still has matched activity and cost on an inert
route.

**Mechanism shown.** The aligned candidate relation is not a surviving
single-write effect or label effect.

**Function established.** Two contribution operations can jointly constitute
one later-relevant carrier condition. Because quantity-matched one-source
replacement succeeds, the result is jointness of operations/inputs, not
jointness of participant identities.

**Geometric meaning.** The response opportunity is attached to carrier access,
not uniquely to one responder identity.

**Function established.** One common condition can be encountered by more than
one registered eligible responder.

**Claim limit.** This is one access-scope contrast. It is not general transfer,
broadcast, communication, or population-level coordination.

## 6. Perturbation, stability, and persistence

P2-I2 tested several kinds of stability, but not every meaning of the word.

### What was tested

- **Seed replication:** no matched configuration changes across three frozen
  seeds.
- **Order perturbation:** state is invariant; history and hybrid are
  intentionally order-conditioned.
- **State perturbation:** native P debit selectively changes state and hybrid.
- **History perturbation:** H_P reference clamp selectively changes history and
  hybrid.
- **Component perturbation:** the hybrid 2 × 2 factorial separates P and H.
- **Admission perturbation:** diversion prevents common constitution while
  preserving source activity and cost.
- **Partition perturbation:** isolated private carriers do not reproduce the
  common effect.
- **Identity perturbation:** arbitrary label permutation is invariant.
- **Access perturbation:** an alternate eligible responder retains the
  relation.
- **Restoration stability:** save/load, equal-input continuation, reset, and
  paired native/adapter identity reconstruct exactly.

### What was not tested

The main experiment does not establish long-duration persistence, spontaneous
recovery after damage, asymptotic stability, saturation, leakage dynamics,
maintenance cost, replenishment, depletion/recovery cycles, population scale,
or open-ended adaptation. Leakage, saturation, and maintenance are explicitly
not applicable in the registered short window. “Stable” here means exact
reproduction and controlled invariance where expected, not ecological
homeostasis.

## 7. What the main experiment unlocks

Within its claim boundary, main P2-I2 demonstrates a reusable functional
pattern:

```text
multiple attributable physical events
        -> one source-label-independent common carrier
        -> an independently intervenable state/history identity
        -> a later carrier-scoped native response
```

That pattern supplies four concrete capabilities:

1. **Common-state constitution:** independent writes become one present
   condition rather than two mailboxes.
2. **Temporal constitution:** formation order can itself become shared causal
   state.
3. **Component conjunction:** current state and active history can jointly gate
   one later response.
4. **Access decoupling:** response eligibility can follow carrier access rather
   than writer or responder identity.

The state-carried leg shows that PyGRC can natively express the first and
fourth functions in this fixture. The history and hybrid legs identify one
precise missing native building block: a source-label-independent active
shared-history carrier that owns admission, idempotency, ordered mixing,
intervention, readout materialization, restoration, and reset.

## 8. Appendix A: distinct operations composing through a live multi-axis carrier

Appendix A asks a different question from the main experiment. The main
experiment varies how two contributions inhabit a pool. Appendix A asks
whether three physically different operations can jointly create a functional
carrier condition that no valid proper subset can create.

### 8.1 Geometry

Appendix A uses a separate native 12-node, 28-edge PyGRC topology:

```text
S_G -- generative enrichment G --------+
S_E -- leakage-gated extraction E -----+--> four-axis live carrier C
S_P -- redistribution/buffering P -----+      environment
                                               support
matched diversion axes for omissions          distinguishability
                                               boundary
                                                    |
                                                    v
                                      native carrier-only R producer
                                      sends 0.82-fraction packets to
                                      passive noncontributor response node
```

All three participant nodes have matched access to the four common axes and
four isolated diversion axes. Missing operations still execute with matched
actor activity, amounts, timing, packet count, and cost against diversion
paths. The response has carrier-only provenance.

### 8.2 Primary eight-case composition test

Every arm is operationally valid. The question is whether it satisfies the
same four measured gates: environment feedback, support feedback, maximum
phase residual, and route/budget leakage.

| Case | What happens geometrically | Why the full signature fails or passes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Reference | All operations diverted; carrier remains baseline | No environment or support response |
| G | Enriches all axes strongly | Feedback passes, but phase residual is too large (`0.02394`) |
| E | Extracts from an unprepared carrier | Environment and support response are negative |
| P | Redistributes/buffers without sufficient enriched material | Environment and support gains are too small |
| GE | Enrichment is shaped by extraction | Environment and residual pass; support gain (`0.01558`) remains below `0.02` |
| GP | Enrichment plus redistribution remains strong | Phase residual (`0.02682`) remains too large |
| EP | Extraction plus redistribution lacks generative support | Feedback is insufficient and residual also fails |
| GEP | Enrichment, extraction, and redistribution jointly reshape the carrier | All gates pass: environment `0.04674`, support `0.02214`, residual `0.01296`, leakage approximately `5.54e-16` |

### 8.3 Mechanism

The positive result is not merely “three packets are larger than two.” G adds
carrier capacity, E removes and reshapes part of that enriched condition, and P
redistributes/buffers the remaining condition. The complete composition lands
inside a bounded functional region: sufficient environment and support
response without excessive phase residual or route leakage. Different proper
subsets miss different parts of that region.

Carrier clamp removes the response. Withdrawing each operation changes the
later response or its registered mediator, and independently restoring that
mediator restores the complete signature. This warrants bounded functional
complementarity of operations through the carrier.

### 8.4 Participant and order controls

One capacity-relaxed source performing all three operations reproduces the
result. A cyclic participant-to-operation rotation, source-label permutation,
and both tested adjacent order inversions (`EGP` and `GPE`) also reproduce it.

Thus the load-bearing structure in Appendix A is:

```text
the set of three distinct carrier operations and their complete carrier effect
```

It is not:

```text
three distinct participant identities or the unique sequence G -> E -> P
```

### 8.5 Functionality and possible foundations

**Bounded functionality unlocked.** Appendix A demonstrates a native,
multi-stage carrier transformation in which generative, subtractive, and
redistributive operations jointly satisfy a functional envelope that no
proper subset satisfies.

**Possible foundation.** This could ground bounded regulation, multi-stage
resource processing, buffering after extraction, or constraint-satisfying
carrier control. A genuine metabolism, resource economy, maintenance cycle, or
homeostatic system would still need explicit conserved budgets, replenishment,
leakage, persistence, and failure/recovery tests.

## 9. Appendix B: the G/E/P question returned to P2-I2 carriers

Appendix B deliberately returns the abstract G/E/P operations to the exact
P2-I2 carrier architectures. It does not reuse Appendix A outcomes.

### 9.1 Geometry

The same 23-node P2-I2 topology is reused, with three native operation types:

```text
G:    S1 -> P       amount +0.625     generative enrichment
E:    P  -> K_P     amount -0.4375    extraction
P_op: S2 -> P       amount +0.875     redistribution/buffering contribution

physical operation arrivals
   -> current P, ordered active H_C, or both
   -> mode-specific P2-I2 feedback mask
   -> native B response of 0.0 or 0.125
```

`P_op` denotes the redistributive operation; `P` alone denotes the shared pool
node. All coherence changes, history materialization packets, feedback
evaluation, producer decisions, and B packets are native PyGRC operations.
The ordered H_C admission/fold remains adapter-owned.

### 9.2 APP-B1: what was established before scientific execution

APP-B1 bound the native routes, three mode realizations, eight-case proper-
subset estimator, controls, restoration identities, and one 99-arm campaign.
Response-free conformance showed that each operation path and carrier boundary
could execute and restore. It established realizability, not a composition
result.

### 9.3 APP-B2: mode-separated runtime result

The 99 fresh models give one especially revealing comparison:

| Mode | GEP | Strongest proper subset | Margin | Interpretation |
| --- | ---: | --- | ---: | --- |
| State-carried | `0.0` | GP = `0.125` | `-1` | E removes enough current P to suppress a response already reached by GP |
| History-carried | `0.125` | all seven subsets = `0.0` | `1` | GEP is sufficient and every registered proper subset is insufficient; matched three-token alternatives are deferred to APP-B4 |
| Hybrid | `0.125` | P and GP = `0.125` | `0` | The hybrid responds, but E is nonessential; irreducibility fails |

All results repeat across the three frozen seeds.

### 9.4 What the comparison means geometrically

The physical operations are the same in all three modes. Only their carrier
representation changes.

- In **current state**, E is subtractive. GP leaves a stronger present `P`
  condition than GEP, so adding E destroys rather than completes the response.
- In **active history**, E is not only a debit. It is an admitted event in the
  ordered GEP formation pattern. Deleting E from that registered candidate
  leaves GP, whose two-token materialized history does not reach the response
  condition. This establishes proper-subset dependence, not yet identity-
  specific necessity.
- In the **hybrid**, the P operation or GP already supplies enough joint
  state/history signal. Adding E does not create an irreducible dependency.

At the APP-B2 boundary, the important mechanism result is:

> A proper-subset relation is not a fixed property of the physical operations.
> It depends on what the carrier preserves and which matched alternatives the
> comparator includes.

Relative to the registered deletion subsets, history turns a physically
subtractive E event into an apparently necessary temporal constituent. State
treats E as suppression, and the registered hybrid treats it as redundant.
APP-B4 then shows that E identity is not globally necessary in the history
fixture: GGP, GPP, PGP, PEP, and PPP can also respond.

### 9.5 Controls and scope

In the supported history realization:

- reference, G, E, P, GE, GP, and EP are all valid zeros;
- history clamp removes the response;
- state-only intervention is inert;
- equal final state with different ordered history changes the response;
- private partitions do not reproduce it;
- controller-authored response is causally excluded; and
- one-source, cyclic role rotations, and label permutation reproduce it.

At this boundary, the result concerns GEP versus its seven proper subsets
inside one active shared history. Participant plurality remains non-load-
bearing. Whether all three operation identities are necessary is deliberately
left to the matched-cardinality APP-B4 panel.

### 9.6 APP-B3: the exact implication boundary

APP-B3 confirms that the APP-B2 execution itself was sound and that active
history admitted exactly the intended native G/E/P events:

```text
physical admissions = 123 expected / 123 observed
G / E / P admissions = 41 / 41 / 41
unexpected or duplicate admissions = 0
final active tokens = 111 expected / 111 observed
```

It also identifies a real limitation. APP-B2 compared GEP with every proper
subset, but not with a three-event repeated-operation alternative such as GGG,
GGP, or PEP. Thus APP-B2 establishes proper-subset composition, but not that
the distinct operation identities are necessary rather than generic event
cardinality, total dose, or ordered quantitative history.

At the APP-B3 boundary, the honest technical result was therefore a **bounded
history-carried, scaffold-dependent three-operation shared-pool composition
candidate**. Operation complementarity was unresolved there; APP-B4 supplies
the matched-cardinality evidence that resolves that ambiguity below.

### 9.7 APP-B4: the executed three-token discriminator

APP-B4 addresses that missing comparison directly. It executes the 24 ordered
three-token sequences that can run from the unchanged baseline, plus one
reference, at three seeds: 75 fresh PyGRC arms.

All 75 arms are operationally valid, with zero child failure or retry. The
complete aggregate was retained before a final Python/JSON seed-key equality
assertion made the parent exit nonzero. Canonical rebuilt and retained analysis
bytes are identical; retained-only reconstruction and closeout pass without a
campaign rerun or scientific regeneration.

```text
all G/E/P triples = 27
feasible from unchanged baseline = 24
excluded as physically infeasible = EEG, EEE, EEP
```

The exclusions are geometric rather than outcome-based. Pool `P` begins at
`0.75`; one E withdraws `0.4375`, leaving `0.3125`, so an immediate second E
cannot withdraw its registered amount. Those rows are outside the physical
domain and cannot be treated as zeros.

The observed rule is exact across the executable domain and all three seeds:

```text
positive = GGP, GEP, GPP, EGP, EPP, PGP, PEP, PPP
zero     = every feasible triple ending in G or E, plus reference

response = 0.125 if and only if the third token is P
```

Thus raw three-event cardinality is insufficient, GEP is not uniquely
positive, and equal-multiset permutations differ. The exact G/E/P permutation
group makes the order effect visible: GEP and EGP respond, while GPE, EPG,
PGE, and PEG do not. The same terminal-P split appears in GGP, GPP, and EPP
multiset families. Repeated PPP is positive.

Geometrically, ordered source-label-free G/E/P admissions are folded through
the scaffolded active history and materialized at `M_H`. The frozen native
feedback threshold then schedules the B packet only for the terminal-P class.
The result is consistent with a quantitative recency gate in which the newest
high-dose P token crosses threshold after a filled three-admission context.
APP-B2's single P and tested one-/two-operation proper subsets remain zero, so
P alone is not sufficient in those contexts.

The resulting function is a bounded terminal-token-qualified temporal gate,
not distinct-operation complementarity. APP-B4 does not independently
intervene on recency coefficient, dose, fold formula, or threshold, and it
does not impute an outcome to excluded terminal-P sequence EEP.

## 10. How the main result and appendices fit together

The three bodies of work answer progressively different questions:

| Evidence body | Question | Answer |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Main P2-I2 | Can multiple contributions constitute one later-relevant carrier? | Yes, through present state, ordered active history, or both; the exact response relation depends on carrier temporality |
| Appendix A | Can three different carrier operations be jointly functional when no subset is? | Yes, in a separate native four-axis live carrier with a bounded gate signature |
| Appendix B | Can the same abstract G/E/P roles compose through the accepted P2-I2 carrier architectures, and what explains the history result? | Proper-subset composition occurs only in the frozen history realization; APP-B4 resolves it as a three-admission terminal-P history gate rather than distinct-operation complementarity |

This is not yet scientific recurrence. Appendix A and Appendix B use different
geometries, measurements, and authorities. Their descriptive convergence is a
future synthesis input:

```text
carrier organization determines which combinations become functional
```

Appendix A shows a multi-axis present carrier in which opposing operations
jointly land inside a functional envelope and remain individually necessary
under its causal controls. Appendix B shows an active temporal carrier in
which a deletion-subset result initially makes E appear necessary, while the
matched-cardinality panel reveals a simpler terminal-P temporal rule. The
difference is mechanistic evidence, not a conflict: the carriers compute
different functions.

## 12. What could legitimately be based on this

The following are prospective foundations, not established ecological
semantics.

### 12.1 Graph/runtime building blocks

1. A native active shared-history carrier with exact admission, idempotency,
   ordered mixing, clamp/replacement, readout materialization, restoration, and
   reset.
2. First-class common/private carrier partitions and carrier-scoped response
   masks, reducing experiment-composed guard logic.
3. Composite carrier identities that expose current state and active history
   as separately intervenable components.
4. Native receipts for ecological operation roles and multi-axis carrier
   metrics, if similar demands recur across independent experiments.

P2-I2 identifies these as candidate building blocks. Cross-experiment
synthesis must precede implementation priority.

### 12.2 New experiment families

- **Naturalization:** replace `RCAEActiveHistoryAdapterV2` with a graph-native
  carrier while preserving all main history/hybrid controls.
- **APP-B4 mechanism intervention:** vary recency, P dose, token count, fold,
  and threshold separately to identify which coupled feature is load-bearing.
- **Scale:** increase contributors, operation types, or eligible responders
  while keeping source labels out of the causal read.
- **Economy:** add explicit reserve depletion, replenishment, leakage,
  saturation, and maintenance over longer windows.
- **Robustness:** test damage, delayed intervention, recovery, repeated cycles,
  and stochastic perturbation rather than only exact deterministic replay.
- **Transfer:** move the same carrier function across topology, timescale, or
  domain without importing thresholds or conclusions.
- **Cross-lane synthesis:** compare the active-carrier demand with trails,
  support fields, boundary exchange, and capacity circulation only after those
  lanes close independently.

### 12.3 Possible higher-level systems

With new evidence, these mechanisms could become parts of:

- shared threshold or eligibility systems;
- source-label-independent temporal pattern detectors;
- state-and-history qualified control surfaces;
- multi-stage generative/extractive/redistributive processing circuits;
- bounded shared environmental context for several local processes; or
- restorable common-state components in larger reflexive-coherence systems.

P2-I2 does not itself establish communication, cooperation, coordination,
memory in the semantic sense, metabolism, agency, collective identity, an
ecological motif, or a regime. Those names require additional controlled
functions, not merely reinterpretation.

## 13. Overall interpretation

P2-I2's deepest contribution is not the scalar response `0.125`. It is a set
of causal distinctions for shared geometry:

```text
common versus private
physical event versus attribution label
present state versus formation history
carrier-mediated response versus output-equivalent bypass
operation composition versus participant plurality
proper-subset irreducibility versus matched-cardinality explanation
native expression versus scaffold-dependent realization
```

Together these distinctions show how a shared medium can become functionally
specific without being anthropomorphized. A carrier can accumulate, remember
order, conjunct state with history, admit several operations, and expose a
later opportunity to another eligible responder. Which combinations become
functional depends on the carrier's geometry and temporality.

The immediate implementation lesson is precise: native current-state
composition is already adequate in the bounded fixture; active shared history
is the recurring missing causal leg inside main P2-I2 and Appendix B. The
immediate scientific lesson is equally precise: carrier organization can
change a physical operation from suppressive, to deletion-subset-necessary,
to redundant—and matched-cardinality evidence can reveal that apparent
necessity as a terminal-token rule instead. That is a strong basis for
subsequent controlled work, while remaining below claims of cooperation,
ecology organization, or life.
