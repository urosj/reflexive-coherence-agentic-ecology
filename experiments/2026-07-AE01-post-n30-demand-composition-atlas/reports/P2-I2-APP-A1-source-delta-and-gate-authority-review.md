## What the retained N29 evidence actually is

N29 I14.1 and I14.3 copy retained N28 neighborhood-capacity traces into new
runtime-candidate artifacts; they do not run the present PyGRC packet runtime.
I14.2-3 preserves the extractive source deltas and applies an explicit producer
leakage factor of `0.5`. I14.5-2 then adds the retained G/E/P axis deltas,
multiplies the positive sum by `0.82`, derives the residual arithmetically, and
records merge leakage as the declared constant `0.018`.

Those artifacts remain the correct semantic source for generative enrichment,
leakage-gated extraction, processor redistribution/buffering, and later
changed-medium response. They are not live measurements in the coherence,
packet, event-time, or topology domain exposed by source-current LGRC9V3.

## What source-current PyGRC can do natively

The current public runtime has a model-owned packet/event ledger, deterministic
packet departure and arrival processing, arrival local updates, causal flux
routes, a native source-coherence-fraction producer, feedback surfaces,
snapshot/save/load, restoration identity, reset-baseline persistence, and
deterministic continuation.

That is sufficient for the load-bearing realization:

- `G`: a distinct registered source node sends native packets into the shared
  carrier axes;
- `E`: the carrier sends native packets to a distinct registered extraction
  sink;
- `P`: a distinct registered processor hub receives and redistributes native
  packets through the same carrier;
- `R`: after the operation sequence, the native causal-flux producer reads
  only carrier-node coherence and sends `0.82` source-coherence-fraction
  packets to a passive noncontributor response node.

An experiment observer still has to assign the source-grounded `G/E/P` labels
and calculate the four Appendix metrics from native receipts. That observer is
read-only and is not a load-bearing producer. This exposes a useful PyGRC gap:
the carrier transitions are native, while the N29 ecological-role and
capacity/phase/leakage measurement vocabulary is not.
