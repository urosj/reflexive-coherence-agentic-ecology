## Role

This roadmap will govern the Post-N30 Agentic-Ecology Demand and Composition
Atlas. The atlas is a bounded local experiment program with specification-like
contracts. It is neither a canonical specification nor graph experiment N31.

Its core question is provisionally:

> What ecology-side shared-medium patterns can be consumed, composed, or
> constructed from the N29/N30 handoff, which substrate distinctions remain
> missing or unsuitable, and which recurring demands—if any—justify a later
> graph-side experiment?

The graph project supplies source-current LGRC evidence and owns native
substrate implementation and graph-side experiment authority. RCAE consumes
those surfaces read-only, runs ecology-side composition and demand probes, and
may return versioned demand contracts. A future graph result may then be
consumed through a new explicit RCAE binding. No work governed by this roadmap
may modify the graph/PyGRC repository.

## Initial lane scope

AE01 begins with seven independently executable lanes:

1. minimal shared-medium niche formation;
2. shared-pool co-conditioning;
3. trail or stigmergic field;
4. nursery or support field;
5. boundary-conditioned exchange;
6. capacity circulation; and
7. parent-basin modulation demand and missing-surface classification.

These names identify questions, not supported results. Lane 7 is deliberately
allowed to close as a missing-surface demand rather than a positive M3/M4
result. Cross-lane synthesis remains closed until every lane has a valid
terminal classification under the frozen common contract.

## Phase 1 freeze obligations

Before lane execution, this roadmap and the AE01 contract set must freeze:

- source roles, precedence, digests, and claim ceilings;
- stable lane IDs, current display names, motivations, hypotheses, terminal
  classifications, and stopping rules;
- one machine-readable lane registry with validated roadmap and README
  projections and explicit rename provenance;
- common pattern, medium, requirement, composition, debt, and claim records;
- constructed-mechanism necessity, minimality, counterfactual, withdrawal,
  debt, and graph-discriminator declarations;
- runtime-binding receipts and replay-frozen realization profiles;
- catalog layer, domain role, field applicability, and transfer boundaries;
- positive, negative, blocked, incomplete, and unsafe-relabel controls;
- artifact roles, manifests, canonicalization, reconstruction, and reports;
- graduated evidence-use tiers, resolved shared profiles, and generated live
  execution receipts;
- N31+ ranking, sensitivity, tie, and non-selection rules; and
- AE01-C0 through AE01-C6 evidence and closeout gates.

Detailed roadmap sections will be completed and reviewed in P1-I2. This
scaffold records accepted scope only and must not be cited as atlas evidence.

## Claim boundary

This roadmap does not establish a primitive, building block, composition,
motif, regime, domain ecology, coordination mechanism, agency claim, or N31+
selection. Constructed RCAE mechanisms remain constructed, and conceptual
domain examples remain non-evidential until dedicated probes classify them.
